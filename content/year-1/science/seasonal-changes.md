# Seasonal Changes — Year 1 Science

## Big Question
Why do the seasons change, and how does the weather differ in each one?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about the **four seasons**, how **daylight hours** change throughout the year, and typical **weather patterns** for each season.

## Key Science Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| season | one of four times of the year — spring, summer, autumn, winter |
| weather | what it is like outside (sunny, rainy, windy, snowy…) |
| daylight | the hours when the sun is up and it is light outside |
| temperature | how hot or cold something is |

## The Science Explained

### The Four Seasons

| Season | Months (UK) | Weather | Daylight |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🌸 Spring | March – May | warmer, showers, breezy | getting longer |
| ☀️ Summer | June – August | warm/hot, sunny | longest days |
| 🍂 Autumn | September – November | cooler, windy, rainy | getting shorter |
| ❄️ Winter | December – February | cold, frost, snow | shortest days |

### What Changes?
- **Temperature** goes up in summer and down in winter.
- **Daylight hours** are longest in summer (around 16 h in the UK) and shortest in winter (around 8 h).
- **Trees** grow leaves in spring, are full in summer, lose leaves in autumn, and are bare in winter.
- **Animals** hibernate in winter and are active in spring and summer.

## Quick Quiz
1. Name the four seasons in order starting with spring. → *Spring, summer, autumn, winter*
2. Which season has the longest days? → *Summer*
3. In which season do many trees lose their leaves? → *Autumn*
4. What is the weather often like in winter? → *Cold, frosty, sometimes snowy*

## Science in the Real World
Farmers plant crops in spring and harvest in autumn. We wear different clothes for each season — t-shirts in summer, warm coats in winter. Weather forecasters help us know what each day will be like.
