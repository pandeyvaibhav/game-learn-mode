# Everyday Materials — Year 1 Science

## What We're Learning
You are going to learn about different materials and their properties. You will find out why certain materials are used to make certain objects.

## Key Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| Material | What an object is made from |
| Property | What a material is like (hard, soft, bendy) |
| Wood | A hard material that comes from trees |
| Metal | A strong, shiny material (like iron or steel) |
| Plastic | A light material that can be shaped |
| Glass | A hard, see-through material |
| Fabric | A soft material used to make clothes |

## Let's Explore

### Common materials

| Material | Properties | Objects made from it |
|---|---|---|
| Wood | Hard, strong, natural | Table, door, pencil |
| Metal | Hard, strong, shiny | Spoon, coin, key |
| Plastic | Light, waterproof, bendy | Bottle, toy, ruler |
| Glass | Hard, transparent (see-through), smooth | Window, mirror, jar |
| Fabric | Soft, flexible, can be cut | T-shirt, curtain, towel |
| Paper | Thin, light, can be torn | Book, card, newspaper |
| Rock | Very hard, heavy, natural | Wall, pebble, statue |

### Properties of materials

| Property | What it means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Hard | Difficult to squash | Rock, metal |
| Soft | Easy to squash | Fabric, sponge |
| Stretchy | Can be pulled longer | Elastic, rubber |
| Stiff | Does not bend easily | Wood, metal |
| Bendy | Can be bent | Plastic, paper |
| Waterproof | Does not let water through | Plastic, metal |
| Transparent | You can see through it | Glass, clear plastic |

### Example — Why is a window made of glass?
Because glass is **transparent** — you can see through it!

### Example — Why is a coat made of waterproof fabric?
Because it keeps the rain out and you stay dry!

## Did You Know? 🔬
Glass is made from sand! When sand is heated to a very high temperature it melts and can be shaped. When it cools down, it becomes the hard, see-through glass we know.
