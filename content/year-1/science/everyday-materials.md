# Everyday Materials — Year 1 Science

## Big Question
What are everyday objects made from, and why?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about common **materials** — wood, plastic, glass, metal, and water — and describe their **properties** (what they look and feel like).

## Key Science Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| material | what something is made from |
| property | a word that describes a material (hard, soft, shiny…) |
| wood | a natural material from trees |
| plastic | a man-made material — light and waterproof |
| glass | a see-through material that can break |
| metal | a strong, shiny material (iron, steel, aluminium) |

## The Science Explained

### Common Materials and Their Properties
| Material | Properties | Example objects |
|---|---|---|
| Wood | hard, strong, natural | table, pencil, door |
| Plastic | light, waterproof, bendy | bottle, toy, ruler |
| Glass | transparent, smooth, fragile | window, jar, mirror |
| Metal | strong, shiny, cold to touch | spoon, coin, key |
| Fabric | soft, flexible, can be cut | t-shirt, curtain, towel |

### Describing Properties
Use these words to describe materials:
hard, soft, rough, smooth, shiny, dull, transparent (see-through), opaque (not see-through), waterproof, absorbent, stretchy, stiff.

### Why Does Material Matter?
A window is made of **glass** because we need to see through it.
A coat is made of **waterproof fabric** to keep us dry.
A bridge is made of **metal** because it must be very strong.

## Quick Quiz
1. Name three everyday materials. → *Wood, plastic, glass (or metal, fabric)*
2. Why is glass used for windows? → *Because it is transparent (see-through)*
3. Which material is waterproof and light? → *Plastic*
4. Is wood a natural or man-made material? → *Natural*

## Science in the Real World
Engineers choose the best material for every job. Car bodies use metal for strength, tyres use rubber for grip, and windows use glass so drivers can see the road.
