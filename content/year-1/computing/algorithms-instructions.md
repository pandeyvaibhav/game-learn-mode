# Algorithms and Instructions — Year 1 Computing

## Big Question
What is an algorithm, and why does the order of instructions matter?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn that an **algorithm** is a set of clear steps in the right order. You will practise giving and following instructions.

## Key Computing Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| algorithm | a set of step-by-step instructions to do something |
| instruction | a single direction telling you what to do |
| sequence | the correct order of steps |
| debug | finding and fixing a mistake in your instructions |

## The Computing Explained

### What Is an Algorithm?
An algorithm is like a recipe — it tells you what to do, step by step, in the right order.

**Example — Making a Jam Sandwich**
1. Get two slices of bread.
2. Spread butter on one slice.
3. Spread jam on the butter.
4. Put the other slice on top.
5. Cut in half.

If you do step 3 before step 1, it won't work! The **order** matters.

### Giving Clear Instructions
Instructions must be **clear** and **exact**:
- ❌ "Go over there." (where?)
- ✅ "Walk forward 5 steps, then turn left."

### Debugging
If the result is wrong, look at your steps — one of them might be in the wrong place or missing. Fixing it is called **debugging**.

## Quick Quiz
1. What is an algorithm? → *A set of step-by-step instructions*
2. Why does the order of steps matter? → *If steps are in the wrong order, the result will be wrong*
3. What is debugging? → *Finding and fixing a mistake in instructions*
4. Put these in order: [spread jam / get bread / eat sandwich / put slices together] → *Get bread → spread jam → put slices together → eat sandwich*

## Computing in the Real World
Sat-navs use algorithms to find the best route. Robots follow algorithms to build cars in factories. Even traffic lights follow an algorithm — red, amber, green!

- [ ] I can put instructions in the correct order
