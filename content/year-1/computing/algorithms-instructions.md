# Algorithms and Instructions — Year 1 Computing

## What We're Learning
You are going to learn what an **algorithm** is. An algorithm is a set of step-by-step instructions to complete a task. You will practise putting instructions in the right order and making them clear.

## Key Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| Algorithm | A set of step-by-step instructions |
| Instruction | A single step that tells you what to do |
| Sequence | Steps in the correct order |
| Debug | Finding and fixing a mistake in instructions |
| Input | Something you put in (like pressing a button) |
| Output | Something that comes out (like a sound or picture) |

## Let's Explore

### What is an algorithm?
An algorithm is like a recipe. It tells you **what to do** and **in what order**.

### Example — Making a sandwich 🥪

```
Step 1: Get two slices of bread.
Step 2: Spread butter on one slice.
Step 3: Put cheese on the butter.
Step 4: Put the other slice on top.
Step 5: Cut in half.
```

The order matters! You cannot put cheese on before the bread.

### Example — Getting dressed

```
Step 1: Put on your pants.
Step 2: Put on your trousers.
Step 3: Put on your socks.
Step 4: Put on your shoes.
```

What happens if you put shoes on before socks? It would not work!

### Debugging — Fixing mistakes
These instructions are wrong. Can you spot the mistake?

```
Step 1: Pour the juice.
Step 2: Get a glass. ← This should be first!
Step 3: Drink the juice.
```

Fixed:
```
Step 1: Get a glass.
Step 2: Pour the juice.
Step 3: Drink the juice.
```

### Giving clear directions

```
"Go that way" ← Not clear!
"Take 3 steps forward, then turn left" ← Clear! ✓
```
