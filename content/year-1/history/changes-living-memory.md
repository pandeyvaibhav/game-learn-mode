# Changes Within Living Memory — Year 1 History

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn how everyday life has **changed**. You will compare toys, transport and technology from the **past** with what we have **now**.

## Key Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| past | a time before now |
| present | the time we live in now |
| memory | something you remember |
| transport | how people travel (car, bus, train) |
| technology | machines and gadgets people use |
| compare | look at how things are the same or different |

## Let's Explore

### Then and Now
| Topic | In the Past | Now |
|---|---|---|
| Toys 🧸 | Wooden blocks, rag dolls, spinning tops | Tablets, LEGO sets, remote-control cars |
| Transport 🚗 | Horse and cart, steam trains | Cars, electric trains, aeroplanes |
| Technology 📱 | No televisions, no internet | Smartphones, computers, video calls |
| School 🏫 | Chalk and slate boards | Whiteboards, tablets, computers |

### What Grandparents Remember
Your grandparents did not have the internet when they were children. They played outside, listened to the radio, and wrote letters instead of sending messages.

## Try It Yourself

**1. What does "the past" mean?**
<details><summary>Answer</summary>A time before now</details>

**2. Name a toy children played with long ago.**
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Wooden blocks (or rag dolls, spinning tops)</details>

**3. How did people travel before cars were invented?**
<details><summary>Answer</summary>By horse and cart (or walking, steam train)</details>

**4. Do children today use chalk and slate in school?**
<details><summary>Answer</summary>No — they use whiteboards and computers now</details>

**5. What is one thing that has stayed the same?**
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Children still play, go to school and have families</details>

## Did You Know?
The first car was built in 1886 by Karl Benz. It had only three wheels and went about as fast as you can jog!
