# Phonics — Phase 3 — Year 1 English

## Big Question
How do two letters work together to make one sound?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about **digraphs** — two letters that team up to make a single sound. You will practise consonant digraphs like **ch**, **sh**, and **th**, and vowel digraphs like **ai**, **ee**, and **oa**.

## Key English Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| phonics | using letter sounds to read and spell words |
| digraph | two letters that make one sound (e.g. sh → /sh/) |
| consonant digraph | a digraph made from two consonants |
| vowel digraph | a digraph that includes a vowel sound |
| blend | say the sounds in a word smoothly together to read it |
| segment | break a word into its sounds to spell it |

## The English Explained

### Consonant Digraphs
| Digraph | Sound | Example Words |
|---|---|---|
| ch | /ch/ | chip, chat, much |
| sh | /sh/ | ship, shop, fish |
| th | /th/ | this, that, with |

### Vowel Digraphs
| Digraph | Sound | Example Words |
|---|---|---|
| ai | long a | rain, tail, snail |
| ee | long e | tree, bee, sheep |
| oa | long o | boat, coat, road |
| oo | long oo | moon, food, spoon |
| ow | /ow/ | cow, now, town |

### Reading with Digraphs
When you see a digraph, say it as **one sound**:
- **sh** + **i** + **p** → ship
- **r** + **ai** + **n** → rain
- **t** + **r** + **ee** → tree

## Quick Quiz
1. What is a digraph? → *Two letters that make one sound*
2. What sound does "sh" make? → */sh/ as in ship*
3. Read this word: b-oa-t → *Boat*
4. Which digraph is in the word "sheep"? → *ee*
5. Segment the word "chip" into sounds. → *ch-i-p*

## English in the Real World
Every time you read a book, you use phonics! Knowing digraphs helps you read words like "children" and "birthday" without getting stuck.

## Learning Checklist
- [ ] I can read words with ch, sh, and th
- [ ] I can read words with ai, ee, and oa
- [ ] I can blend sounds together to read new words
