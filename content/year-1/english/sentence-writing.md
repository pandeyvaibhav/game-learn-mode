# Sentence Writing — Year 1 English

## Big Question
What does every good sentence need?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn the rules for writing a great sentence. Every sentence needs a **capital letter** at the start, **finger spaces** between words, and a **full stop** at the end. You will also learn to join ideas using **and**.

## Key English Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| sentence | a group of words that makes sense on its own |
| capital letter | a big letter used to start a sentence or a name (A, B, C…) |
| full stop | a dot (.) at the end of a sentence |
| finger space | a gap between words so they don't squash together |
| conjunction | a joining word, like **and** |

## The English Explained

### The Sentence Checklist
Every sentence needs:
1. **Capital letter** at the start → The
2. **Finger spaces** between words → The ␣ cat ␣ sat
3. **Makes sense** — the reader understands it
4. **Full stop** at the end → The cat sat.

### Using "and"
You can join two ideas with **and**:
- I like apples. I like oranges.
- → I like apples **and** oranges.

### Spot the Mistakes
❌ the dog is big → needs a capital letter: **The** dog is big.
❌ Thedogisbig. → needs finger spaces: **The dog is big.**
❌ The dog is big → needs a full stop: The dog is big **.**

## Quick Quiz
1. What goes at the start of every sentence? → *A capital letter*
2. What goes at the end of every sentence? → *A full stop*
3. Fix this sentence: "i like cats" → *I like cats.*
4. Join these: "I can run. I can jump." → *I can run and jump.*

## English in the Real World
We write sentences in letters, stories, messages, and even shopping lists! Good sentences help people understand exactly what you mean.

## Learning Checklist
- [ ] I start every sentence with a capital letter
- [ ] I put finger spaces between my words
- [ ] I end every sentence with a full stop
- [ ] I can use "and" to join two ideas
