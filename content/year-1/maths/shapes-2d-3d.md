# 2D and 3D Shapes — Year 1 Maths

## Big Question
What shapes can you see all around you?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn the names of **flat shapes** (2D) and **solid shapes** (3D). You will find out how to describe them by counting sides, corners, and faces.

## Key Maths Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| 2D shape | a flat shape with length and width (like a drawing) |
| 3D shape | a solid shape with length, width, and height (like an object you can hold) |
| side | a straight line on a 2D shape |
| corner (vertex) | where two sides meet |
| face | a flat surface on a 3D shape |
| edge | where two faces of a 3D shape meet |

## The Maths Explained

### 2D Shapes (Flat)
| Shape | Sides | Corners | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Circle | 0 (curved) | 0 | Clock face |
| Triangle | 3 | 3 | Roof of a house |
| Square | 4 (all equal) | 4 | Window |
| Rectangle | 4 (2 long, 2 short) | 4 | Door |

### 3D Shapes (Solid)
| Shape | Faces | Edges | Corners | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sphere | 0 (curved) | 0 | 0 | Ball |
| Cube | 6 | 12 | 8 | Dice |
| Cuboid | 6 | 12 | 8 | Cereal box |
| Cylinder | 2 circles + 1 curved | 2 | 0 | Tin of beans |
| Cone | 1 circle + 1 curved | 1 | 1 (point) | Ice cream cone |

## Quick Quiz
1. How many sides does a triangle have? → *3*
2. What 3D shape looks like a ball? → *Sphere*
3. How many corners does a square have? → *4*
4. How many faces does a cube have? → *6*
5. Name a 2D shape with no corners. → *Circle*

## Maths in the Real World
Shapes are everywhere! Wheels are circles, boxes are cuboids, and ice cream cones are… cones! Builders use rectangles for bricks, and footballs are made from a mix of pentagons and hexagons.

## Learning Checklist
- [ ] I can name 2D shapes and count their sides
- [ ] I can name 3D shapes and count their faces
- [ ] I can spot shapes in the world around me
