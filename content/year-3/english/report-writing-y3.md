# Report Writing — Year 3 English

## What We're Learning
You are going to learn how to write a non-chronological report — a text that gives facts about a topic without telling a story. You will use **sub-headings**, a **formal tone** and **facts** to organise your writing clearly.

## Key Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| Report | A text that gives information and facts about a topic. |
| Non-chronological | Not in time order — organised by topic instead. |
| Sub-heading | A smaller heading that introduces a section. |
| Formal tone | Writing that sounds serious and informative, not chatty. |
| Fact | Something that is true and can be checked. |
| Topic sentence | The first sentence of a paragraph that tells you what it is about. |

## Let's Explore

### Structure of a Report

A report usually looks like this:

```
Title
Introduction — what the report is about
Sub-heading 1 — first main point
Sub-heading 2 — second main point
Sub-heading 3 — third main point
Conclusion — a summary or interesting final fact
```

### Formal vs Informal

| Informal (chatty) | Formal (report) |
|---|---|
| Cats are really cool pets. | Cats are popular domestic animals. |
| They eat loads of stuff. | They eat a variety of foods including meat and fish. |
| It's dead interesting! | This is a fascinating fact. |

In a report, avoid slang and keep the tone serious.

### Example Report

> **Hedgehogs**
>
> **Introduction**
> Hedgehogs are small mammals found across Europe. They are easy to recognise because of their sharp spines.
>
> **Appearance**
> A hedgehog has around 5,000 spines on its back. Its belly is covered in soft fur. Adults are about 25 cm long.
>
> **Diet**
> Hedgehogs eat insects, slugs and worms. They are nocturnal, which means they hunt at night.
>
> **Habitat**
> They live in hedgerows, gardens and woodland edges. In winter they hibernate in piles of leaves.

Notice: sub-headings, facts, formal tone, no "I think" or "I like".

# Report Writing — Year 3 English

## What We're Learning
You are going to learn how to write a **non-chronological report** — a piece of writing that gives facts and information about a topic. You will practise using **sub-headings**, **formal language**, and the **present tense** to organise your writing clearly.

## Key Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| Report | A piece of writing that informs the reader about a topic |
| Non-chronological | Not in time order — organised by topic instead |
| Sub-heading | A smaller heading that introduces a new section |
| Formal tone | Writing that sounds serious and professional (no slang) |
| Present tense | Writing about what happens now ("Elephants live…" not "Elephants lived…") |
| Fact | Something that is true and can be checked |

## Let's Learn Together

### What Makes a Good Report?

A report is different from a story. It **informs** — it does not entertain with characters and plot.

```
TITLE                      ← bold, tells you the topic
Introduction               ← what is this report about?
Sub-heading 1              ← first area of information
  paragraph of facts…
Sub-heading 2              ← next area
  paragraph of facts…
Interesting fact / summary ← a memorable ending
```

### Model Report

> **Hedgehogs**
>
> **What are hedgehogs?**
> Hedgehogs are small, nocturnal mammals covered in sharp spines. They live in gardens, hedgerows, and woodlands across Europe.
>
> **What do they eat?**
> Hedgehogs mainly eat insects, slugs, and worms. They use their excellent sense of smell to find food at night.
>
> **How do they protect themselves?**
> When a hedgehog feels threatened, it curls into a tight ball. Its spines point outward, making it difficult for predators to attack.
>
> **Did you know?**
> A hedgehog has around 5 000 spines on its body. Each one lasts about a year before it falls out and a new one grows in its place.

Notice: sub-headings as questions, present tense ("live", "eat", "curls"), formal tone, and facts.
