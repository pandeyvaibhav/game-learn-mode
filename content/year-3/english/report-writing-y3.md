# Report Writing — Year 3 English

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn how to write a **non-chronological report** — a piece of writing that gives facts and information about a topic. You will practise using **sub-headings**, **formal language**, and the **present tense** to organise your ideas clearly.

## Key Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| report | a piece of writing that informs the reader about a topic |
| non-chronological | not in time order — organised by topic instead |
| sub-heading | a smaller heading that introduces a section |
| formal tone | writing that sounds serious and professional (no slang) |
| present tense | writing about what happens now ("Elephants live…" not "Elephants lived…") |
| fact | something that is true and can be checked |

## Let's Learn Together

### What makes a good report?

A report is different from a story. It **informs** — it doesn't entertain with characters and plot. Look at this structure:

```
TITLE                      ← bold, tells you the topic
Introduction               ← what is this report about?
Sub-heading 1              ← first area of information
  paragraph of facts…
Sub-heading 2              ← next area
  paragraph of facts…
Interesting fact / summary ← a memorable ending
```

### Model report

> **Hedgehogs**
>
> **What are hedgehogs?**
> Hedgehogs are small, nocturnal mammals covered in sharp spines. They live in gardens, hedgerows, and woodlands across Europe.
>
> **What do they eat?**
> Hedgehogs mainly eat insects, slugs, and worms. They use their excellent sense of smell to find food at night.
>
> **How do they protect themselves?**
> When a hedgehog feels threatened, it curls into a tight ball. Its spines point outward, making it very difficult for predators to attack.
>
> **Did you know?**
> A hedgehog has around 5,000 spines on its body — and each one lasts about a year before it falls out and is replaced!

Notice: sub-headings, present tense ("live", "eat", "curls"), formal tone, and facts.

## Your Turn

Write a short report (3 paragraphs) about an animal of your choice. Use this frame:

1. **Introduction** — What is the animal? Where does it live?
2. **What does it eat?** — Describe its diet.
3. **An amazing fact** — One surprising detail.

**Remember to:**
- Use sub-headings
- Write in the present tense
- Keep a formal tone
