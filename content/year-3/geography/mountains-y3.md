# Mountains — Year 3 Geography

## Where in the World?
Mountains are found on every continent. The **Himalayas** stretch across Asia, the **Andes** run down the western side of South America, and the **Alps** cross eight countries in Europe. In the UK, the highest mountains are **Ben Nevis** in Scotland (1 345 m), **Yr Wyddfa (Snowdon)** in Wales (1 085 m), and **Scafell Pike** in England (978 m).

## What We're Learning
You are going to find out how mountains are formed, explore their different parts from valley to summit, and discover why the climate changes as you climb higher.

## Key Geography Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| Mountain | A very high area of land, usually over 600 m above sea level |
| Mountain range | A chain of mountains joined together |
| Summit | The very top of a mountain |
| Peak | A pointed mountain top |
| Fold mountain | A mountain made when rocks are pushed up and folded by tectonic plates |
| Volcanic mountain | A mountain built from lava and ash from eruptions |
| Altitude | Height above sea level |
| Climate | The usual weather pattern in a place over a long time |
| Vegetation | The plants that grow in a place |
| Erosion | When wind, water, or ice wear rock away over time |

## The Big Picture

### How Mountains Are Made

Most mountains take millions of years to form.

**Fold mountains** form when two tectonic plates push towards each other. The land between them crumples and rises — a bit like pushing a rug from both ends. The Himalayas and the Alps are fold mountains.

**Volcanic mountains** grow when lava and ash build up after many eruptions. Mount Fuji in Japan is a famous volcanic mountain.

**Block mountains** form when giant blocks of rock are pushed up along cracks in the Earth's crust.

### Climbing Up a Mountain

Imagine walking from the bottom to the top:

1. **Valley** — the warmest part, where people may farm or build villages.
2. **Forest zone** — trees and shrubs can still grow here.
3. **Above the treeline** — too cold for most trees; only low plants survive.
4. **Summit** — the air is cold, thin, and often covered in snow.

As you climb higher, the temperature drops by about 1 °C for every 150 m. That is why some mountain tops stay snowy even in summer.

### Famous Mountains and Ranges
- **Mount Everest** is the tallest mountain on Earth at 8 849 m.
- **The Andes** in South America are the longest mountain range in the world.
- **The Alps** stretch across eight countries in Europe.
- **Ben Nevis** is the tallest mountain in the UK at 1 345 m.

## People and Places
People have lived in mountain areas for thousands of years. In the Himalayas, communities farm on steep terraces cut into the hillside and keep yaks for milk and transport. In the Alps, villages attract visitors for hiking in summer and skiing in winter, so tourism is an important industry. Mountain communities must also prepare for dangers like avalanches and landslides.

## Fascinating Facts
- In 1953, Tenzing Norgay and Edmund Hillary were the first people confirmed to reach the summit of Everest.
- The Andes stretch over 7 000 km — roughly the distance from London to New York and back.
- Mountain goats can climb nearly vertical cliff faces thanks to special hooves with soft, gripping pads.
