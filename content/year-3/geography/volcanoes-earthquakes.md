# Volcanoes and Earthquakes — Year 3 Geography

## What We're Learning
You are going to find out why the Earth sometimes shakes and why mountains can explode with lava! You will learn about **tectonic plates**, how **volcanoes** erupt, and what causes **earthquakes**.

## Key Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| Tectonic plates | Huge pieces of the Earth's surface that fit together like a jigsaw and move very slowly. |
| Volcano | An opening in the Earth where hot melted rock (magma) can escape. |
| Magma | Hot liquid rock below the surface. When it reaches the surface it is called lava. |
| Lava | Magma that has come out of a volcano. |
| Eruption | When a volcano explodes or pushes out lava, ash and gas. |
| Earthquake | A sudden shaking of the ground caused by tectonic plates moving. |

## Let's Explore

### Inside the Earth

The Earth has layers, a bit like an egg:

```
   ╭──────────────╮
   │  Crust (thin) │ ← We live here
   │ ╭───────────╮ │
   │ │  Mantle    │ │ ← Hot, slow-moving rock
   │ │ ╭───────╮ │ │
   │ │ │ Core  │ │ │ ← Very hot metal centre
   │ │ ╰───────╯ │ │
   │ ╰───────────╯ │
   ╰──────────────╯
```

### Tectonic Plates

The crust is broken into giant plates that float on the mantle. They move only a few centimetres a year — but over time this causes big events!

### Volcanoes

Most volcanoes form where plates **meet**:

1. Magma rises through cracks between plates.
2. Pressure builds up underground.
3. The volcano **erupts** — lava, ash and gases burst out.
4. Lava cools and hardens, building up the volcano's shape.

**Types:**
- **Active** — could erupt at any time (e.g. Mount Etna, Italy)
- **Dormant** — sleeping, not erupted recently but could
- **Extinct** — will not erupt again (e.g. Edinburgh Castle sits on one!)

### Earthquakes

When tectonic plates push, pull or slide past each other, energy builds up. When it is released — **SHAKE!**

- The point underground where it starts = **focus**
- The point on the surface directly above = **epicentre**
- We measure earthquakes with the **Richter scale** (higher number = stronger)

# Volcanoes and Earthquakes — Year 3 Geography

## Where in the World?
Volcanoes and earthquakes are found in many countries, but most are clustered along the edges of tectonic plates. The **Ring of Fire** is a horseshoe-shaped zone around the Pacific Ocean — from New Zealand, through Japan, across to the west coast of the Americas. About 75% of the world's volcanoes sit on the Ring of Fire.

## What We're Learning
You are going to look deep inside the Earth to understand **tectonic plates**, discover how **volcanoes** form and erupt, and learn what causes **earthquakes**. You will also explore some of the most famous eruptions in history.

## Key Geography Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| Tectonic plates | Huge slabs of rock that make up the Earth's surface — they float on hot rock below |
| Volcano | An opening in the Earth's surface where hot magma, ash and gas escape |
| Magma | Hot melted rock inside the Earth |
| Lava | Magma that has reached the Earth's surface |
| Eruption | When a volcano releases lava, ash and gas |
| Earthquake | A sudden shaking of the ground caused by tectonic plates moving |
| Crust | The thin outer layer of the Earth |
| Mantle | The thick layer of hot rock beneath the crust |
| Core | The centre of the Earth — extremely hot |

## The Big Picture

### Inside the Earth

The Earth is made up of layers, a bit like an onion:

```
  ╭──────────────────╮
  │     CRUST         │  ← thin, rocky surface (5–70 km)
  │  ╭──────────────╮ │
  │  │   MANTLE      │ │  ← hot, slow-moving rock (2 900 km thick)
  │  │  ╭──────────╮ │ │
  │  │  │  OUTER   │ │ │  ← liquid iron and nickel
  │  │  │  CORE    │ │ │
  │  │  │ ╭──────╮ │ │ │
  │  │  │ │INNER │ │ │ │  ← solid iron and nickel, over 5 000 °C
  │  │  │ │ CORE │ │ │ │
```

### Tectonic Plates

The crust is cracked into enormous pieces called **tectonic plates**. These plates float on the mantle and move very slowly — just a few centimetres per year. Where plates meet, volcanoes and earthquakes are most likely to happen.

### How Volcanoes Form

Volcanoes form where tectonic plates pull apart or push together. Magma rises up through cracks and erupts as **lava** on the surface.

| Type | Shape | Eruption style |
|---|---|---|
| Shield volcano | Wide, gentle slopes | Lava flows gently |
| Composite volcano | Tall, steep, cone-shaped | Explosive with ash and lava |

### Famous Eruptions
- **Mount Vesuvius (AD 79)** — buried the Roman city of Pompeii under ash in Italy.
- **Krakatoa (1883)** — an eruption in Indonesia so loud it was heard nearly 5 000 km away.
- **Mount St. Helens (1980)** — a massive eruption in the USA that removed the top of the mountain.

### How Earthquakes Happen

Earthquakes occur when tectonic plates suddenly slip or collide. The energy released travels through the ground as waves, which cause shaking. The point on the surface directly above is called the **epicentre** — where shaking is strongest.

## People and Places
Scientists called **volcanologists** study volcanoes to help protect people living nearby. Countries on plate boundaries, such as Japan and New Zealand, build special earthquake-resistant structures. Iceland sits on a plate boundary and uses underground heat (geothermal energy) to warm homes and swimming pools.

## Fascinating Facts
- The largest volcano in the solar system is **Olympus Mons** on Mars — it is about 22 km high.
- There are more than 1 500 active volcanoes on Earth.
- Some earthquakes are so small that people do not feel them — scientists detect thousands every day using special instruments.
