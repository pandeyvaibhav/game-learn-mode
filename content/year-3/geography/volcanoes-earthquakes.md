# Volcanoes and Earthquakes вҖ” Year 3 Geography

## Big Question
Why does the Earth shake and why do volcanoes erupt?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will look deep inside the Earth to understand **tectonic plates**, discover how **volcanoes** form and erupt, and learn why **earthquakes** happen. You will also find out about some of the most famous eruptions in history.

## Key Geography Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| tectonic plates | huge slabs of rock that make up the Earth's surface вҖ” they float on hot, liquid rock below |
| volcano | an opening in the Earth's surface where hot magma, ash and gas escape |
| magma | hot melted rock inside the Earth |
| lava | magma that has reached the Earth's surface |
| eruption | when a volcano explodes and throws out lava, ash and gas |
| earthquake | a sudden shaking of the ground caused by tectonic plates moving |
| crust | the thin outer layer of the Earth (like the skin of an apple) |
| mantle | the thick layer of hot rock beneath the crust |
| core | the centre of the Earth вҖ” extremely hot (inner core is solid, outer core is liquid) |

### Tectonic Plates
The crust is broken into enormous pieces called **tectonic plates**. These plates float on the mantle and move very slowly вҖ” just a few centimetres per year. Where plates meet, exciting (and sometimes dangerous) things happen!

### Volcanoes
Volcanoes form where tectonic plates pull apart or push together. Magma rises up through cracks and erupts as **lava** on the surface.

**Types of volcano:**
| Type | Shape | Eruption style |
|---|---|---|
| Shield | Wide, gentle slopes | Lava flows gently |
| Composite (stratovolcano) | Tall, steep, cone-shaped | Explosive with ash and lava |

**The Ring of Fire** вҖ” a horseshoe-shaped zone around the Pacific Ocean where about 75% of the world's volcanoes are found.

### Famous Eruptions
- **Mount Vesuvius (AD 79)** вҖ” buried the Roman cities of Pompeii and Herculaneum under ash.
- **Krakatoa (1883)** вҖ” the explosion was heard nearly 5,000 km away!
- **Mount St. Helens (1980)** вҖ” a massive eruption in the USA that blew the top off the mountain.

### Earthquakes
Earthquakes happen when tectonic plates suddenly slip or collide. The energy released travels through the ground as **seismic waves** вҖ” that is what makes the shaking.

The point underground where the earthquake starts is called the **focus**. The point on the surface directly above is the **epicentre** вҖ” where shaking is strongest.

## Quick Quiz
1. What are tectonic plates? вҶ’ *Huge slabs of rock that make up the Earth's surface*
2. What is the difference between magma and lava? вҶ’ *Magma is inside the Earth; lava is on the surface*
3. Name one famous volcanic eruption. вҶ’ *Vesuvius AD 79 (or Krakatoa 1883, Mount St. Helens 1980)*
4. What causes earthquakes? вҶ’ *Tectonic plates suddenly slipping or colliding*
5. What is the hottest part of the Earth? вҶ’ *The inner core*

## Geography in the Real World
Scientists called **volcanologists** study volcanoes to try to predict eruptions and keep people safe. Japan and New Zealand are on the Ring of Fire, so they have special earthquake-proof buildings. Iceland sits on a plate boundary and uses heat from underground (geothermal energy) to warm homes and swimming pools!
