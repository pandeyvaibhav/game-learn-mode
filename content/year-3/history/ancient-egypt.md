You are going to explore one of the most amazing civilisations ever — **Ancient Egypt**. You will discover how the Egyptians built the pyramids, why they made mummies, and how the River Nile kept everyone alive.

## Let's Explore

### When and Where?

Ancient Egypt lasted over 3,000 years — roughly 3100 BC to 30 BC. It was in north-east Africa, along the **River Nile**.

### The River Nile

The Nile was the **lifeblood** of Egypt:
- Every year it **flooded**, leaving rich black soil perfect for farming.
- It provided **water** for drinking, washing and watering crops.
- It was used as a **highway** — boats carried people and goods.

### The Pyramids

- The **Great Pyramid at Giza** was built around 2560 BC for Pharaoh Khufu.
- It is made of about **2.3 million stone blocks**, each weighing as much as a car.
- Thousands of skilled workers (not slaves as once thought) spent about 20 years building it.

### Mummification

The Egyptians believed in life after death. To prepare a body:

1. **Remove the organs** (stored in special jars).
2. **Dry the body** with salt (natron) for 40 days.
3. **Wrap** in linen bandages — sometimes hundreds of metres!
4. Place in a **sarcophagus** inside the tomb.
5. Include food, jewels and items for the afterlife.

### Hieroglyphics

Egyptian writing used small pictures called **hieroglyphs**. There were over 700 different symbols! The **Rosetta Stone** (found in 1799) helped us decode them.

# Ancient Egypt — Year 3 History

## Big Question
What made Ancient Egypt one of the greatest civilisations in history, and how have we uncovered its secrets?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will explore the fascinating world of **Ancient Egypt** — from powerful pharaohs and enormous pyramids to mysterious mummies and clever hieroglyphics. You will discover why the **River Nile** was the lifeblood of Egyptian civilisation.

## The History Explained

### The River Nile — Lifeline of Egypt
Ancient Egypt grew up along the **River Nile** in north-east Africa. Every year the Nile flooded, leaving behind rich, dark soil perfect for growing crops like wheat and barley. Without the Nile, Egypt would have been nothing but desert.

### Pharaohs
Pharaohs were all-powerful rulers. Egyptians believed they were gods on Earth. Famous pharaohs include:
- **Khufu** — built the Great Pyramid of Giza
- **Hatshepsut** — one of the few female pharaohs
- **Tutankhamun** — the "boy king" who became pharaoh at just 9 years old

### Pyramids
The pyramids were **tombs** built to protect pharaohs in the afterlife. The **Great Pyramid of Giza** is made of over 2 million stone blocks and was the tallest building in the world for nearly 4,000 years!

### Mummies and Mummification
Egyptians believed in life after death. To prepare the body:
1. Organs were removed and stored in special jars (canopic jars).
2. The body was dried out with a salt called **natron** for 40 days.
3. It was wrapped tightly in linen bandages.
4. A painted **death mask** was placed over the face.
5. The mummy was placed inside a **sarcophagus**.

### Hieroglyphics
Instead of an alphabet, Egyptians used **pictures and symbols** to write. There were over **700 different hieroglyphs**! For a long time no one could read them — until the **Rosetta Stone** was discovered in 1799, which had the same text in hieroglyphics, another Egyptian script, and Greek.

### Everyday Life
- **Farmers** grew crops on the Nile's fertile banks.
- **Craftspeople** made pottery, jewellery, and linen clothes.
- **Children** played with toys like dolls, spinning tops, and balls.
- Cats were considered sacred animals!

## History in the Real World
You can see real mummies and Egyptian artefacts at the **British Museum** in London. The pyramids at Giza are still standing after over 4,500 years — they are one of the **Seven Wonders of the Ancient World** (and the only one still standing!). Hieroglyphics inspired many modern ideas about codes and symbols.
