# Perimeter — Year 3 Maths

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will find out what **perimeter** means and how to measure it. You will add up the lengths around the outside of shapes — including tricky rectilinear shapes (shapes made only of right angles). By the end you will be able to calculate the perimeter of any 2D shape when you know its side lengths.

## Key Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| perimeter | the total distance around the outside of a shape |
| 2D shape | a flat shape (like a square, rectangle, or triangle) |
| rectilinear | a shape made only of straight lines and right angles |
| length | how long something is, measured in cm, m, etc. |
| right angle | a square corner — exactly 90° |

## Let's Explore

Imagine walking around the edge of a football pitch. The total distance you walk is the **perimeter**.

To find the perimeter, **add up all the side lengths**.

```
    5 cm
  ┌───────┐
  │       │ 3 cm
  └───────┘
    5 cm
```

Perimeter = 5 + 3 + 5 + 3 = **16 cm**

### Example 1 — Rectangle

A rectangle has a length of 8 cm and a width of 4 cm.

```
      8 cm
  ┌──────────┐
  │          │ 4 cm
  └──────────┘
```

Perimeter = 8 + 4 + 8 + 4 = **24 cm**

**Shortcut:** A rectangle has two pairs of equal sides, so you can also calculate:
(8 + 4) × 2 = 12 × 2 = **24 cm** ✓

### Example 2 — Rectilinear shape (L-shape)

```
  ┌────┐
  │    │ 2 cm
  │    └────┐
  │         │ 3 cm
  └─────────┘
  4 cm   2 cm
```

All sides: 2 cm + 2 cm + 3 cm + 4 cm + 5 cm + 2 cm

Wait — we need to work out the missing sides!
- Left side = 2 + 3 = **5 cm**
- Top + bottom step = 2 + 2 = **4 cm** (same as the bottom)

Perimeter = 2 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + 2 = **18 cm**

## Try It Yourself

1. Find the perimeter of a square with sides of 6 cm.
2. A rectangle is 10 cm long and 3 cm wide. What is the perimeter?
3. A triangle has sides 5 cm, 7 cm and 8 cm. What is the perimeter?
4. An equilateral triangle has all sides of 9 cm. What is the perimeter?
5. A rectilinear shape has these side lengths: 2 cm, 4 cm, 3 cm, 2 cm, 5 cm, 6 cm. What is the perimeter?

## Did You Know? 🧮
The word "perimeter" comes from the Greek words **peri** (around) and **metron** (measure). The perimeter of the whole Earth around the equator is about **40,075 km** — that's like driving from London to Edinburgh about 60 times!
