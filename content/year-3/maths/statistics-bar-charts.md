# Statistics — Bar Charts — Year 3 Maths

## What We're Learning
You are going to learn how to read and draw bar charts and pictograms. These show information using bars and pictures so you can compare things quickly.

## Key Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| Data | Information that has been collected. |
| Bar chart | A chart using bars of different heights to show amounts. |
| Pictogram | A chart using small pictures to show amounts. |
| Axis | A line along the side or bottom of a chart. |
| Scale | Numbers on an axis that tell you what each line means. |
| Tally | Counting using groups of five marks. |

## Let's Explore

### Example 1 — Reading a bar chart

Favourite fruits in a class:

```
Number of children
 8 |        ████
 7 |        ████
 6 |  ████  ████
 5 |  ████  ████  ████
 4 |  ████  ████  ████
 3 |  ████  ████  ████  ████
 2 |  ████  ████  ████  ████
 1 |  ████  ████  ████  ████
   +------+------+------+------
    Apple  Banana Orange  Pear
```

- Apple: 6 — Banana: 8 — Orange: 5 — Pear: 3
- Most popular? **Banana** (tallest bar)

### Example 2 — Reading a pictogram

Each 📖 = **2 books**:

```
Asha : 📖 📖 📖      = 6 books
Ben  : 📖 📖          = 4 books
Cara : 📖 📖 📖 📖   = 8 books
Dev  : 📖 📖 📖       = 6 books
```

Most books? **Cara** (8). Asha vs Ben? 6 − 4 = **2 more**.

### Example 3 — Answering questions

From the fruit chart:
- Apple + Pear = 6 + 3 = **9**
- Banana − Orange = 8 − 5 = **3 more**
- Total = 6 + 8 + 5 + 3 = **22**

## Try It Yourself

Each ⭐ = **2 goals**:

```
Team A : ⭐ ⭐ ⭐
Team B : ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐
Team C : ⭐ ⭐
Team D : ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐
```

1. How many goals did Team B score?
2. Which team scored fewest?
3. How many more goals: Team D vs Team A?
4. Total goals by all teams?

2. Team C (2 × 2 = 4 goals)

4. 6 + 8 + 4 + 10 = 28 goals

</details>

## Did You Know? 🧮
The first bar chart was drawn by William Playfair, a Scottish man, in 1786. He also invented pie charts! We still use his ideas over 200 years later.

# Statistics — Bar Charts — Year 3 Maths

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn how to **read** and **draw** bar charts and pictograms. These are clever ways to show data so you can compare things at a glance.

## Key Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| data | information that has been collected (like numbers or answers) |
| bar chart | a chart that uses bars (rectangles) to show data — taller bars mean bigger numbers |
| pictogram | a chart that uses pictures or symbols to show data |
| axis | a line along the side or bottom of a chart (plural: axes) |
| scale | the numbers along an axis that tell you what each line means |
| tally | a way of counting using groups of five marks |

## Let's Explore

A bar chart makes data easy to compare. The bars can go up (vertical) or sideways (horizontal).

Here is data about favourite colours in a class:

| Colour | Votes |
|---|---|
| Blue | 9 |
| Red | 6 |
| Green | 4 |
| Yellow | 7 |

As a bar chart:

```
Votes
 9 | ████
 8 |
 7 | ████                    ████
 6 | ████  ████            ████
 5 | ████  ████            ████
 4 | ████  ████  ████      ████
 3 | ████  ████  ████      ████
 2 | ████  ████  ████      ████
 1 | ████  ████  ████      ████
   +------+------+------+------
    Blue    Red   Green  Yellow
```

You can see straight away that **Blue** got the most votes because its bar is the tallest.

### Example 1 — Reading a bar chart

Look at the chart above.

**Q:** How many more people chose Blue than Green?
**A:** Blue = 9, Green = 4, so 9 − 4 = **5 more**.

### Example 2 — Drawing a pictogram

Using the same data with ☺ = 2 votes:

```
Apple:   ☺☺☺☺     (4 symbols = 8)
Banana:  ☺☺½      (2½ symbols = 5)
Orange:  ☺☺☺      (3 symbols = 6)
Grapes:  ☺½       (1½ symbols = 3)
```

Notice that half a symbol means half the value of a full one!

## Try It Yourself

Use this bar chart data — pets owned by Year 3 children:

| Pet | Children |
|---|---|
| Dog | 10 |
| Cat | 7 |
| Fish | 4 |
| Rabbit | 6 |

1. Which pet is the most popular?
2. How many children have a cat?
3. How many more children have a dog than a fish?
4. How many children were asked altogether?
5. If each symbol in a pictogram stands for 2 children, how many symbols would you draw for Rabbit?

<details>
<summary>Check your answers</summary>

1. **Dog** (10 children)
2. **7 children**
3. 10 − 4 = **6 more**
4. 10 + 7 + 4 + 6 = **27 children**
5. 6 ÷ 2 = **3 symbols**

</details>

## Did You Know? 🧮
The first bar chart was drawn by a Scottish man called **William Playfair** in 1786 — that's over 230 years ago! He also invented pie charts. Before his clever idea, people could only read tables of numbers.
