# Fractions вҖ” Year 3 Maths

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will discover how fractions work. You will learn about **unit fractions** and **non-unit fractions**, and practise adding and subtracting fractions that share the same denominator. You will see fractions everywhere вҖ” from slicing cakes to sharing fruit!

## Key Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| fraction | a part of a whole вҖ” written with a top and bottom number |
| numerator | the top number вҖ” tells you how many parts you have |
| denominator | the bottom number вҖ” tells you how many equal parts the whole is split into |
| unit fraction | a fraction with 1 on top, like В№вҒ„вӮғ or В№вҒ„вӮ… |
| non-unit fraction | a fraction with more than 1 on top, like ВІвҒ„вӮ… or ВівҒ„вӮ„ |
| equal parts | pieces that are exactly the same size |

## Let's Explore

A fraction describes **part of a whole**. When you cut a cake into 4 equal slices, each slice is **В№вҒ„вӮ„** (one quarter) of the cake.


- The **denominator** (bottom) tells you how many equal parts: **4**
- The **numerator** (top) tells you how many parts you have: **2**
- So two shaded parts = **ВІвҒ„вӮ„**

### Example 1 вҖ” Naming a fraction

A melon is cut into 6 equal slices. You eat 2 slices.

- Denominator = **6** (total slices)
- Numerator = **2** (slices eaten)
- Fraction eaten = **ВІвҒ„вӮҶ**

This is a **non-unit fraction** because the numerator is more than 1.
