# Plants Functions — Year 3 Science

## Big Question
What do plants need to grow, and how do they make new plants?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will find out how plants make their own food through **photosynthesis**, follow the journey of a plant's **lifecycle**, and discover the amazing ways plants spread their seeds.

## Key Science Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| photosynthesis | the process plants use to make food from sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide |
| roots | the underground part that absorbs water and nutrients from the soil |
| stem | carries water from the roots to the leaves and supports the plant |
| leaves | where photosynthesis happens — they capture sunlight |
| pollination | when pollen moves from one flower to another (by insects, wind, or water) |
| seed dispersal | the way seeds travel away from the parent plant to grow somewhere new |
| lifecycle | the stages a plant goes through from seed to adult plant |

## The Science Explained

### How Plants Make Food — Photosynthesis

Plants are amazing because they **make their own food**! They do not eat like animals. Instead they use a process called **photosynthesis**:

```
  Sunlight  +  Water  +  Carbon Dioxide  →  Glucose (food)  +  Oxygen
     ☀️          💧          🌬️                  🍬               🌿
```

- **Sunlight** is captured by the green leaves.
- **Water** is absorbed by the roots and travels up the stem.
- **Carbon dioxide** enters through tiny holes in the leaves.
- The plant combines them to make **glucose** (sugar for energy) and releases **oxygen** — the gas we breathe!

### Parts of a Plant
| Part | Job |
|---|---|
| Roots | Anchor the plant; absorb water and minerals |
| Stem | Transport water up; hold the plant upright |
| Leaves | Capture sunlight for photosynthesis |
| Flower | Makes pollen and seeds for reproduction |

### Pollination

For a plant to make seeds, **pollen** must travel from the stamen (male part) of one flower to the carpel (female part) of another. This is called **pollination**.

**Who helps?**
- 🐝 Insects (bees, butterflies) — bright, scented flowers attract them.
- 💨 Wind — grasses and trees release light pollen into the breeze.

### Seed Dispersal

Once seeds are made, they need to travel **away** from the parent plant so they have space, light, and water to grow. Seeds travel in different ways:

| Method | How it works | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Wind | Light seeds with "wings" or "parachutes" | Dandelion, sycamore |
| Animal | Hooked seeds stick to fur; fruits are eaten and seeds come out later | Burdock, berries |
| Water | Seeds float to a new place | Coconut |
| Explosion | Pod bursts open and flings seeds | Pea pod |

### The Plant Lifecycle
1. **Seed** — a tiny package containing a baby plant and stored food.
2. **Germination** — the seed absorbs water and a root pushes down, a shoot pushes up.
3. **Growth** — the stem, leaves, and roots grow bigger using photosynthesis.
4. **Flowering** — flowers form, attracting pollinators.
5. **Pollination & seed production** — seeds develop inside the flower.
6. **Seed dispersal** — seeds spread and the cycle begins again!

## Explore It! — Investigation Idea
**Question:** What do plants need to grow well?
**You need:** Four small pots with soil, cress seeds, water, a dark cupboard
**What to do:**
1. Plant cress seeds in all four pots.
2. Pot A — water + sunlight. Pot B — no water + sunlight. Pot C — water + dark cupboard. Pot D — no water + dark cupboard.
3. Wait one week and observe.
**What to notice:** Only Pot A grows well — plants need BOTH water and light.

## Quick Quiz
1. What three things do plants need for photosynthesis? → *Sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide*
2. Which part of the plant absorbs water? → *The roots*
3. Name one animal that helps with pollination. → *Bees (or butterflies)*
4. How does a dandelion seed travel? → *By wind*
5. What is germination? → *When a seed starts to grow — a root and shoot emerge*

## Science in the Real World
Without photosynthesis, there would be no oxygen for animals to breathe and no food for us to eat! Farmers think carefully about pollination — many hire beekeepers to bring beehives into their orchards so bees can pollinate fruit trees. And next time you blow a dandelion clock, you are helping with seed dispersal!
