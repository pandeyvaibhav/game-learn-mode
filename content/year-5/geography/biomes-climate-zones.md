# Biomes & Climate Zones — Year 5 Geography

## Big Question
What are biomes and how does climate affect the plants and animals that live there?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about the world's main **biomes** and **climate zones**, and how climate affects **vegetation** and wildlife.

## Key Geography Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| biome | a large area of the world with similar climate, plants, and animals |
| climate zone | a region with a particular pattern of weather over many years |
| tropical | hot and wet all year round, near the equator |
| temperate | moderate temperatures with four seasons |
| polar | extremely cold, near the North and South Poles |
| desert | very dry (less than 250 mm rain per year), can be hot or cold |
| vegetation | the plant life of a particular region |

## The Geography Explained

### Major Biomes
| Biome | Climate | Vegetation | Animals |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tropical Rainforest | Hot, wet all year | Dense forest, tall trees | Monkeys, parrots, jaguars |
| Desert | Very dry, hot days / cold nights | Cacti, scrub | Camels, lizards, scorpions |
| Temperate Forest | Warm summers, cool winters | Deciduous trees (lose leaves) | Deer, foxes, owls |
| Grassland (Savanna) | Warm, dry and wet seasons | Grass, scattered trees | Lions, elephants, zebras |
| Polar / Tundra | Extremely cold, ice and snow | Mosses, lichens | Polar bears, penguins |
| Taiga (Boreal Forest) | Cold, long winters | Coniferous trees (evergreen) | Wolves, bears, moose |

### Climate Zones
- **Tropical** — between the Tropics of Cancer and Capricorn.
- **Temperate** — between the tropics and the polar circles.
- **Polar** — above the Arctic Circle and below the Antarctic Circle.

### Why Does Climate Vary?
- Distance from the **equator** (latitude).
- **Altitude** — higher places are colder.
- Distance from the **sea** — coasts are milder.

## Quick Quiz
1. What is a biome? → *A large area with similar climate, plants, and animals*
2. Which biome is hot and wet all year? → *Tropical rainforest*
3. What makes a desert a desert? → *Very little rainfall (less than 250 mm per year)*
4. Name the three main climate zones. → *Tropical, temperate, polar*

## Geography in the Real World
Climate change is shifting biome boundaries. Understanding biomes helps scientists protect endangered habitats and plan for food production.
