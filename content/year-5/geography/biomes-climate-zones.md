# Biomes and Climate Zones 🎯

## What We're Learning
We're exploring the world's major biomes and climate zones. You'll discover how climate — temperature and rainfall — shapes the landscapes, plants and animals found in different parts of the world, from scorching tropical rainforests to freezing polar regions.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| biome | A large area of the world with a particular climate, vegetation and wildlife |
| climate zone | A region with similar weather patterns over a long period of time |
| tropical | The warm, wet zone near the equator |
| vegetation | The plant life found in a particular area |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### What Are Biomes and Climate Zones?
A biome is a large region of the Earth defined by its climate, plants and animals. Climate zones are bands around the Earth based on temperature and rainfall. Near the equator lies the tropical zone — hot and wet all year. Moving towards the poles, we pass through the subtropical, temperate and polar zones, each getting cooler. The UK sits in the temperate zone with mild, changeable weather.

### Major World Biomes
Key biomes include: **tropical rainforest** (hot, wet, incredibly biodiverse), **desert** (very dry, extreme temperatures), **grassland/savanna** (warm with seasonal rain, home to grazing animals), **temperate forest** (moderate climate with deciduous and evergreen trees), **taiga/boreal forest** (cold, with coniferous trees like pine and spruce) and **tundra** (extremely cold, frozen ground, very few trees). Each biome has plants and animals specially adapted to its conditions.

### How Climate Shapes Life
Plants and animals are adapted to survive in their biome. Cacti store water in their thick stems to survive desert droughts. Polar bears have thick fur and a layer of blubber for Arctic cold. Tropical rainforest trees grow tall to reach sunlight through the dense canopy. Understanding biomes helps us see why protecting habitats around the world is so important — if the climate changes, the biome and its wildlife are affected.

## Did You Know? 🌟
Tropical rainforests cover only about 6% of the Earth's surface, but they are home to more than half of all the world's plant and animal species!
