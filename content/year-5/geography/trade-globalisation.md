# Trade and Globalisation 🎯

## What We're Learning
We're finding out how countries around the world trade with each other. You'll learn what imports and exports are, how goods travel along trade routes and supply chains, and why fair trade matters for workers in developing countries.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| import | A good or service brought into a country from abroad |
| export | A good or service sent from one country to be sold in another |
| trade route | A path or network along which goods are transported between countries |
| fair trade | A system that ensures producers in developing countries receive a fair price for their goods |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Imports and Exports
No country can produce everything its people need, so countries trade with each other. The UK imports goods it cannot easily grow or make — like bananas, coffee and electronics — and exports goods and services it is strong at, such as financial services, medicines and machinery. Trade connects countries and means we can enjoy products from all around the world.

### Trade Routes and Supply Chains
Goods travel along trade routes by ship, plane, lorry and train. A supply chain is the journey a product takes from raw material to your hands. For example, a chocolate bar's supply chain might start with cacao farmers in Ghana, go to a factory in Belgium for processing, and then to a shop in the UK. Supply chains can be very long, crossing many countries.

### Fair Trade
Not everyone in a supply chain is treated fairly. Some farmers and workers in developing countries receive very low pay. Fair trade aims to fix this by guaranteeing a minimum price, better working conditions and money for community projects like schools and clean water. When you see the Fairtrade mark on a product, it means the producers have been paid a fair price. Choosing Fairtrade products is one way to make a difference.

## Did You Know? 🌟
Around 90% of everything traded internationally travels by sea! The largest container ships can carry over 20,000 containers — if you lined them up, they'd stretch for more than 120 kilometres.
