# Trade & Globalisation — Year 5 Geography

## Big Question
How are countries connected through trade and what does globalisation mean for our daily lives?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about **imports** and **exports**, how **supply chains** work, and what **fair trade** means.

## Key Geography Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| trade | buying and selling goods between people or countries |
| import | goods brought into a country from abroad |
| export | goods sent from a country to sell abroad |
| globalisation | the way countries are becoming more connected through trade, travel, and technology |
| supply chain | the journey a product takes from raw material to the shop shelf |
| fair trade | a system ensuring farmers and workers in poorer countries get a fair price |

## The Geography Explained

### Why Do Countries Trade?
- No single country has **all** the resources it needs.
- Some countries have climates suited to growing certain crops (e.g. bananas, cocoa).
- Trade allows countries to **specialise** in what they do best.

### Imports and Exports
| UK Imports (buys from abroad) | UK Exports (sells abroad) |
|---|---|
| Bananas from Central America | Cars and engines |
| Coffee from Brazil / Ethiopia | Medicine and chemicals |
| Electronics from China / Japan | Financial services |
| Oil from Middle East / Norway | Music and entertainment |

### Supply Chains
A chocolate bar's journey:
1. **Cocoa beans** grown in West Africa.
2. **Shipped** by cargo ship to Europe.
3. **Processed** in a factory into chocolate.
4. **Packaged** and transported to shops.
5. **Sold** to the customer.

### Fair Trade
- Farmers in developing countries sometimes receive very low prices.
- **Fair trade** guarantees a **minimum price** and better working conditions.
- Look for the **Fairtrade logo** on products like chocolate, bananas, and coffee.

## Quick Quiz
1. What is an import? → *Goods brought into a country from abroad*
2. What is globalisation? → *Countries becoming more connected through trade, travel, and technology*
3. Why do countries trade? → *No country has all the resources it needs*
4. What does fair trade guarantee? → *A fair minimum price and better working conditions for farmers*

## Geography in the Real World
Almost everything you use has been part of a global supply chain. Your phone, clothes, and food likely came from several different countries. Fairtrade products are available in most supermarkets.
