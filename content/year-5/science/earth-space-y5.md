# Earth and Space 🎯

## What We're Learning
We're exploring our place in the solar system. You'll learn about the planets, understand why we have day and night because of the Earth's rotation, and discover why the Moon appears to change shape throughout the month.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| solar system | The Sun and all the planets, moons and other objects that orbit it |
| rotation | The spinning of the Earth on its axis, which takes approximately 24 hours |
| orbit | The path an object takes around another object in space, e.g. Earth around the Sun |
| moon phase | The different shapes the Moon appears to be as it orbits the Earth |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### The Solar System
Our solar system has eight planets orbiting the Sun: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. The inner four are rocky planets; the outer four are gas and ice giants. The Sun is a star — a huge ball of hot gas that provides light and heat. Earth is the only planet known to support life, thanks to liquid water and a protective atmosphere.

### Earth's Rotation and Day and Night
The Earth spins on its tilted axis once every 24 hours. The side facing the Sun has daytime, while the side facing away has night-time. As the Earth continues to rotate, different parts of the world move into and out of sunlight. This is why the Sun appears to move across the sky — it's actually the Earth that's spinning!

### Moon Phases
The Moon orbits the Earth roughly once every 28 days. It doesn't produce its own light — it reflects sunlight. As the Moon moves around Earth, we see different amounts of its sunlit side. These are the phases: new moon, crescent, first quarter, gibbous, full moon, and then back again. The cycle repeats every month.

**Question 1:** How many planets are in our solar system? Name them in order from the Sun.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Eight: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune.</details>

**Question 2:** What causes day and night?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>The Earth rotating (spinning) on its axis. The side facing the Sun has day; the opposite side has night.</details>

**Question 3:** How long does it take the Earth to complete one full rotation?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Approximately 24 hours (one day).</details>

**Question 4:** Why does the Moon appear to change shape?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>As the Moon orbits Earth, we see different amounts of its sunlit surface, creating the phases.</details>

**Question 5:** Is the Sun a planet or a star?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>The Sun is a star.</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
You could fit about 1.3 million Earths inside the Sun! Despite being so far away (150 million kilometres), the Sun's light reaches us in just over 8 minutes.
