# Earth & Space — Year 5 Science

## Big Question
How does the Earth move through space and what else is in our solar system?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about the **planets** in our **solar system**, how the **Earth rotates** to cause day and night, and why the **Moon** appears to change shape.

## Key Science Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| solar system | the Sun and all the objects that orbit it |
| planet | a large body that orbits a star |
| orbit | the path an object takes around another object |
| rotate | to spin on an axis |
| axis | an imaginary line through the centre of the Earth from pole to pole |
| moon phase | the changing shape of the lit part of the Moon we can see |

## The Science Explained

### The Solar System
Our solar system has **8 planets** orbiting the **Sun**:

| Order | Planet | Key Fact |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mercury | Closest to the Sun, very hot in the day |
| 2 | Venus | Hottest planet (thick atmosphere) |
| 3 | Earth | The only planet known to support life |
| 4 | Mars | Known as the Red Planet |
| 5 | Jupiter | The largest planet |
| 6 | Saturn | Famous for its rings |
| 7 | Uranus | Tilted on its side |
| 8 | Neptune | The furthest planet, very cold |

Memory aid: **M**y **V**ery **E**xcited **M**other **J**ust **S**erved **U**s **N**achos.

### Day and Night
- The Earth **rotates** (spins) on its axis once every **24 hours**.
- The side facing the Sun has **day**; the opposite side has **night**.
- The Earth does NOT stop spinning — different places experience day and night at different times.

### Moon Phases
The Moon orbits Earth roughly every **28 days**. We see different amounts of the lit side:
- **New Moon** — not visible
- **Crescent** — thin sliver
- **Half Moon (Quarter)** — half lit
- **Full Moon** — fully lit

The Moon does not produce its own light — it **reflects** sunlight.

## Quick Quiz
1. How many planets are in our solar system? → *8*
2. Why do we have day and night? → *The Earth rotates on its axis*
3. Which planet is the largest? → *Jupiter*
4. Does the Moon make its own light? → *No, it reflects sunlight*

## Science in the Real World
Space agencies like NASA and ESA send probes to explore other planets. Satellites orbiting Earth help with weather forecasting, GPS, and communications.
