# Forces — Year 5 Science

## Big Question
What forces act on objects and how do they affect movement?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about **gravity**, **air resistance**, **friction**, and simple **mechanisms** like levers and pulleys.

## Key Science Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| force | a push or pull that can change the movement or shape of an object |
| gravity | the force that pulls objects towards the centre of the Earth |
| air resistance | the force of air pushing against a moving object |
| friction | the force between two surfaces rubbing together |
| mechanism | a device that uses forces to make work easier (e.g. lever, pulley, gear) |
| Newton (N) | the unit used to measure force |

## The Science Explained

### Gravity
- Gravity pulls everything **towards the centre of the Earth**.
- It is why objects fall when dropped.
- Weight is a measure of the force of gravity on an object (measured in Newtons).

### Air Resistance
- When an object moves through air, the air **pushes against** it.
- A larger surface area means **more** air resistance (parachutes use this).
- Streamlined shapes reduce air resistance.

### Friction
- Friction acts between **two surfaces** in contact.
- Rough surfaces create **more** friction; smooth surfaces create **less**.
- Friction can be useful (grip on shoes) or unhelpful (slowing a bicycle).

### Mechanisms
| Mechanism | How it helps |
|---|---|
| Lever | A bar that pivots to lift heavy loads with less effort |
| Pulley | A wheel and rope that changes the direction of a force |
| Gear | Toothed wheels that transfer movement and change speed |

## Quick Quiz
1. What is gravity? → *The force that pulls objects towards the centre of the Earth*
2. What increases air resistance? → *A larger surface area*
3. What causes friction? → *Two surfaces rubbing together*
4. Name one simple mechanism. → *Lever / pulley / gear*

## Science in the Real World
Engineers design cars and planes to be streamlined to reduce air resistance. Brakes use friction to slow vehicles. Cranes use pulleys to lift heavy loads on building sites.
