# Forces 🎯

## What We're Learning
We're investigating forces — pushes and pulls that affect how objects move. You'll learn about gravity, air resistance, water resistance and friction, and explore how simple mechanisms like levers, pulleys and gears help us use forces more effectively.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| gravity | The force that pulls objects towards the Earth (or any large mass) |
| air resistance | A type of friction caused by air pushing against a moving object |
| friction | A force between two surfaces that slows movement down |
| mechanism | A device that uses forces to make work easier, e.g. levers, pulleys, gears |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Gravity
Gravity is the force that pulls everything towards the centre of the Earth. It's why objects fall when you drop them and why we stay on the ground. Sir Isaac Newton famously began thinking about gravity after watching an apple fall from a tree. Weight is a measure of the force of gravity acting on an object — that's why we measure it in newtons (N).

### Air Resistance, Water Resistance and Friction
When an object moves through the air, air resistance acts against it — that's why parachutes slow people down. Water resistance works the same way in water, which is why streamlined shapes move more easily through it. Friction happens when two surfaces rub together, like shoes on the ground. Friction can be useful (stopping you from slipping) or unhelpful (wearing out machine parts).

### Mechanisms
Simple mechanisms let us use smaller forces to have a bigger effect. A lever (like a seesaw) lets you lift a heavy object with less effort. Pulleys use wheels and rope to change the direction or size of a force. Gears are interlocking wheels with teeth — a large gear turning a small one makes the small one spin faster. These mechanisms are found everywhere, from bicycles to cranes.

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** What is gravity?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Gravity is the force that pulls objects towards the Earth (or towards any object with mass).</details>

**Question 2:** Why do parachutes slow a skydiver down?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>The large surface of the parachute increases air resistance, which pushes against the direction of movement and slows the fall.</details>

**Question 3:** Give an example where friction is helpful.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Example: The grip on the soles of your shoes stops you from slipping on wet ground.</details>

**Question 4:** What is the difference between air resistance and water resistance?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Air resistance acts on objects moving through air; water resistance acts on objects moving through water. Both oppose the direction of movement.</details>

**Question 5:** How does a lever help us?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>A lever allows us to lift or move a heavy object using less force by increasing the distance over which the force is applied.</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
Without air resistance, a feather and a bowling ball dropped from the same height would hit the ground at exactly the same time! Astronaut David Scott proved this on the Moon in 1971.
