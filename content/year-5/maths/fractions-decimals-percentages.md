# Fractions, Decimals and Percentages 🎯

## What We're Learning
We're discovering how fractions, decimals and percentages are all connected. They are different ways of writing the same value, and being able to switch between them is a powerful maths skill. By the end, you'll be able to convert between all three with confidence.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| equivalent fraction | A fraction that has the same value as another, e.g. ½ = 2/4 |
| decimal | A number written using a decimal point to show tenths, hundredths, etc. |
| percentage | A number out of 100, shown with the % symbol |
| denominator | The bottom number of a fraction — the total number of equal parts |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Equivalent Fractions
Equivalent fractions look different but have the same value. You can find them by multiplying or dividing the numerator and denominator by the same number. For example, 1/2 = 2/4 = 5/10 = 50/100. Drawing fraction walls or bar models can help you see why these are equal.

### Converting Fractions to Decimals
To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator. For example, 3/4 = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75. Some common ones to learn by heart: 1/2 = 0.5, 1/4 = 0.25, 1/5 = 0.2, 1/10 = 0.1. If the denominator is 10 or 100, the conversion is even simpler — just use place value columns.

### Percentage of Amounts
"Per cent" means "out of 100." To find 10% of a number, divide it by 10. To find 1%, divide by 100. You can build other percentages from these. For example, to find 35% of 200: 10% = 20, so 30% = 60; 5% = 10; therefore 35% = 70. This method is useful for real-life problems like discounts and sales.

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** Write 3/5 as a decimal.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>3 ÷ 5 = 0.6</details>

**Question 2:** What is 25% of 80?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>25% = one quarter, so 80 ÷ 4 = 20</details>

**Question 3:** Write two fractions that are equivalent to 2/3.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>4/6 and 6/9 (multiply numerator and denominator by 2 or 3)</details>

**Question 4:** Convert 0.45 to a percentage.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>0.45 × 100 = 45%</details>

**Question 5:** A shirt costs £60. It is reduced by 15%. How much does it cost now?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>10% of £60 = £6, 5% = £3, so 15% = £9. £60 − £9 = £51</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
The word "percent" comes from the Latin *per centum*, meaning "for every hundred." Ancient Romans used fractions based on 100 long before the % symbol was invented!
