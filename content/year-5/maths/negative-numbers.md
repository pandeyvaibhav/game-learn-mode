# Negative Numbers 🎯

## What We're Learning
We're exploring numbers below zero — negative numbers! You'll learn to place them on a number line, count forwards and backwards through zero, compare and order them, and see how they appear in real life, especially with temperature.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| negative number | A number less than zero, written with a minus sign, e.g. −3 |
| positive number | A number greater than zero |
| zero | The boundary between positive and negative numbers |
| integer | A whole number that can be positive, negative or zero |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### The Number Line
Imagine a number line stretching left and right. Zero is in the middle. Positive numbers go to the right (1, 2, 3…) and negative numbers go to the left (−1, −2, −3…). The further left you go, the smaller the number. So −5 is less than −2, even though 5 looks bigger than 2.

### Counting Through Zero
When you count backwards past zero, you enter the negatives: 3, 2, 1, 0, −1, −2, −3. Going forwards from a negative works the same way: −4, −3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2. You can also find the difference between a negative and positive number by counting the steps through zero. The difference between −3 and 4 is 7 steps.

### Temperature and Real Life
Temperature is the most common place you'll see negative numbers. Water freezes at 0 °C. A temperature of −6 °C means six degrees below freezing. If it is −4 °C in the morning and warms up by 9 degrees, the new temperature is 5 °C. Negative numbers also appear in bank balances, sea level (below sea level) and even lifts that go to underground floors.

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** Put these numbers in order from smallest to largest: 3, −1, 0, −5, 2.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>−5, −1, 0, 2, 3</details>

**Question 2:** The temperature is −3 °C. It rises by 7 degrees. What is the new temperature?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>−3 + 7 = 4 °C</details>

**Question 3:** Which is colder: −2 °C or −8 °C?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>−8 °C (it is further below zero)</details>

**Question 4:** What is the difference between −4 and 6?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>10 (count 4 steps from −4 to 0, then 6 steps from 0 to 6)</details>

**Question 5:** Count back 5 from 2. What number do you land on?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>2, 1, 0, −1, −2, −3 → you land on −3</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
The coldest temperature ever recorded on Earth was −89.2 °C at Vostok Station in Antarctica in 1983. That's cold enough to freeze carbon dioxide out of the air!
