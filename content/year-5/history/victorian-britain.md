# Victorian Britain — Year 5 History

## Big Question
How did life in Britain change during the Victorian era?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about **Queen Victoria**, the **Industrial Revolution**, **workhouses**, and key **inventions** that transformed daily life.

## Key History Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| Victorian era | the period when Queen Victoria ruled (1837–1901) |
| Industrial Revolution | the move from hand-made goods to machine-made goods in factories |
| workhouse | a place where the very poor lived and worked in harsh conditions |
| factory | a building where goods are made by machines |
| empire | a group of countries ruled by one nation |
| reform | a change in law or practice to improve people's lives |

## The History Explained

### Queen Victoria
- Became queen in **1837** aged just 18.
- Reigned for **63 years** — the longest until Queen Elizabeth II.
- During her reign, Britain became the most powerful nation in the world.

### The Industrial Revolution
- People moved from **countryside to cities** to work in factories.
- New machines made goods **faster and cheaper**.
- Cities grew rapidly but conditions were often **dirty and overcrowded**.

### Workhouses
- Very poor people, including children, were sent to **workhouses**.
- Conditions were harsh — long hours, little food, families split up.
- Charles Dickens wrote about workhouse life in *Oliver Twist*.

### Key Victorian Inventions
| Invention | Inventor / Date |
|---|---|
| Railways (passenger) | George Stephenson, 1825 |
| Telephone | Alexander Graham Bell, 1876 |
| Light bulb (practical) | Thomas Edison / Joseph Swan, 1879 |
| Flushing toilet (widespread) | Thomas Crapper popularised it |

### Reforms
- Laws were passed to stop **young children** working in factories and mines.
- **Education** became compulsory for all children by 1880.
- Public health improved with better sewers and clean water.

## Quick Quiz
1. When was the Victorian era? → *1837–1901*
2. What was the Industrial Revolution? → *The move from hand-made to machine-made goods in factories*
3. What was a workhouse? → *A place where the poor lived and worked in harsh conditions*
4. Name one Victorian invention. → *Railways / telephone / light bulb*

## History in the Real World
Many buildings, railways, and sewers built in the Victorian era are still in use today. The reforms they introduced laid the foundations for modern workers' rights and education.
