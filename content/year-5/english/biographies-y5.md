# Biographies 🎯

## What We're Learning
We're learning how to read and write biographies — factual accounts of someone else's life. You'll discover how biographies are organised in chronological order, written in the third person and past tense, and use formal language to give a serious, respectful tone.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| biography | A true account of a person's life written by someone else |
| chronological order | Events arranged in the order they happened, from earliest to latest |
| third person | Writing about someone using "he," "she" or "they" rather than "I" |
| formal language | Serious, professional language without slang or casual expressions |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### What Makes a Biography?
A biography tells the story of a real person's life. It includes key facts: when and where they were born, what they achieved, the challenges they faced and their impact on the world. Unlike an autobiography (written by the person themselves), a biography is written by someone else and uses the third person ("she" / "he").

### Structure and Organisation
Biographies are usually written in chronological order. An opening paragraph introduces who the person is and why they are famous. The middle paragraphs cover their early life, key events and achievements in time order. A closing paragraph sums up their legacy — why they are remembered. Time connectives such as "later," "by 1920" and "after this" help the reader follow the timeline.

### Language Features
Biographies use past tense because they describe events that have already happened: "She trained as a nurse." The tone is formal — avoid "awesome" or "cool" and choose words like "remarkable" or "significant." Include precise dates and facts to make the writing reliable and trustworthy.

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** What is the difference between a biography and an autobiography?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>A biography is written by someone else about a person's life. An autobiography is written by the person about their own life.</details>

**Question 2:** Which person (first or third) should a biography be written in?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Third person (using he, she, they).</details>

**Question 3:** Why is chronological order important in a biography?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>It helps the reader follow the events of the person's life in the order they happened.</details>

**Question 4:** Rewrite this sentence in a more formal tone: "Mary Seacole was really cool and helped loads of soldiers."
<details><summary>Answer</summary>"Mary Seacole was a remarkable woman who cared for many wounded soldiers."</details>

**Question 5:** Give two time connectives you could use in a biography.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Examples: "Later," "After this," "By 1860," "Eventually," "During her childhood."</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
One of the oldest biographies ever written is about the Egyptian pharaoh Thutmose III — it was carved into the walls of his temple over 3,400 years ago!
