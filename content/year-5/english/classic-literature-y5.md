# Classic Literature 🎯

## What We're Learning
We're exploring classic literature — books that have stood the test of time and are still loved today. You'll discover famous authors, examine the themes in their stories, look at how the language style differs from modern writing, and think about the historical context in which these books were written.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| classic literature | Books that have been widely read and valued over many years |
| theme | A big idea or message that runs through a story, e.g. friendship, courage |
| language style | The way words and sentences are chosen and arranged by an author |
| historical context | The time period and social conditions when a book was written |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Famous Classic Authors
Some of the most well-known classic authors include Charles Dickens (*Oliver Twist*, *A Christmas Carol*), Lewis Carroll (*Alice's Adventures in Wonderland*), Frances Hodgson Burnett (*The Secret Garden*) and Robert Louis Stevenson (*Treasure Island*). These writers created characters and stories so vivid that people still read and enjoy them today.

### Themes in Classic Stories
Classic books often explore themes that are timeless — good versus evil, friendship, bravery, growing up, and fairness. In *Oliver Twist*, Dickens highlighted the unfair treatment of poor children. In *The Secret Garden*, Burnett wrote about healing and the power of nature. Recognising themes helps you understand the deeper meaning behind a story.

### Language Style and Historical Context
Classic texts often use longer sentences, more formal vocabulary and expressions we wouldn't use today. For example, Dickens might write "It was the best of times, it was the worst of times" rather than simply "Things were mixed." Understanding the historical context — when and where the author lived — helps explain why they wrote in a certain way and what issues mattered to them.

**Question 3:** Why might the language in classic books seem different from modern books?

**Question 4:** What does "historical context" mean?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>It means the time, place and social conditions in which a book was written, which influence its content and style.</details>

<details><summary>Answer</summary>Because the themes — such as courage, friendship and justice — are still relevant, and the stories are so well written they continue to entertain and inspire.</details>
