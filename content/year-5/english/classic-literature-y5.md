# Classic Literature — Year 5 English

## Big Question
What makes a book a "classic" and what can we learn from reading older texts?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn what **classic literature** means, how to explore **themes**, understand the **author's purpose**, and work out **vocabulary in context**.

## Key English Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| classic literature | well-known books that have stood the test of time |
| theme | a big idea or message running through a text (e.g. friendship, bravery) |
| author's purpose | why the author wrote the text (entertain, inform, persuade) |
| context | the words around an unfamiliar word that help you work out its meaning |
| archaic language | old-fashioned words no longer in everyday use |

## The English Explained

### What Is a Classic?
A classic is a book that:
- Has been read and loved for many years.
- Explores themes that are still relevant today.
- Uses rich, powerful language.

### Examples of Classics
| Book | Author | Key Theme |
|---|---|---|
| Alice's Adventures in Wonderland | Lewis Carroll | Curiosity, identity |
| The Secret Garden | Frances Hodgson Burnett | Nature, healing, friendship |
| Oliver Twist | Charles Dickens | Poverty, kindness |
| Treasure Island | Robert Louis Stevenson | Adventure, morality |

### Reading Strategies
1. **Predict** — what might happen based on what you've read so far?
2. **Clarify** — use context clues to understand tricky words.
3. **Question** — why did the author include this scene/character?
4. **Summarise** — what are the key events so far?

### Understanding Archaic Language
Some classics use old-fashioned words:
- *"pray tell"* → please tell me
- *"hence"* → from this place / therefore
- *"dreadful"* → terrible

Use the **surrounding sentences** to work out the meaning.

## Quick Quiz
1. What makes a book a classic? → *It has been loved for many years and explores timeless themes*
2. Name one theme from The Secret Garden. → *Nature / healing / friendship*
3. What does "author's purpose" mean? → *Why the author wrote the text*
4. How can you work out the meaning of an unfamiliar word? → *Use context clues from the surrounding words*

## English in the Real World
Classic books are still adapted into films, plays, and TV shows. Themes like friendship, justice, and bravery remain relevant in modern storytelling.

- [ ] I can identify themes in a classic text
