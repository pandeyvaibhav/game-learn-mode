# Punctuation — Year 2 English

## Big Question
Why do we need punctuation marks in our writing?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn how to use **question marks**, **exclamation marks**, **commas in lists**, and **apostrophes** correctly. Punctuation helps the reader understand your writing.

## Key English Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| punctuation | marks we add to writing to make it clear |
| question mark (?) | goes at the end of a question |
| exclamation mark (!) | shows surprise, excitement, or a command |
| comma (,) | separates items in a list |
| apostrophe (') | shows missing letters or belonging |
| full stop (.) | goes at the end of a sentence |

## The English Explained

### Question Marks ?
Use a **?** when someone is asking something:
- *What is your name?*
- *Can I have a drink?*

### Exclamation Marks !
Use a **!** when someone is excited, surprised, or giving a command:
- *Watch out!*
- *What a lovely day!*

### Commas in Lists
When you list three or more things, use commas:
- I packed a hat**,** a coat**,** and some gloves.
- She likes apples**,** bananas**,** and grapes.

### Apostrophes for Missing Letters
- *do not → don't* (the **o** is missing)
- *I am → I'm* (the **a** is missing)

### Apostrophes for Belonging
- *the dog's bone* → the bone belongs to the dog.
- *Mia's bag* → the bag belongs to Mia.

## Quick Quiz
1. Which punctuation ends a question? → *Question mark (?)*
2. "Wow that is amazing" — which punctuation mark is best? → *Exclamation mark (!)*
3. Add commas: "I bought eggs milk and bread." → *I bought eggs, milk, and bread.*
4. What does the apostrophe show in "didn't"? → *A missing letter (o)*

## English in the Real World
Punctuation is everywhere — in books, signs, messages, and on screen. Without it, writing can be confusing. Imagine reading a whole page with no full stops!

- [ ] I can use commas to separate items in a list
- [ ] I can use apostrophes for missing letters and belonging
