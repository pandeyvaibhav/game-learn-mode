# Punctuation 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning about punctuation marks and how they help our writing make sense. Punctuation tells the reader when to pause, when someone is asking a question, and when something exciting is happening.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| question mark (?) | goes at the end of a question |
| exclamation mark (!) | shows surprise, excitement or a command |
| comma (,) | separates items in a list |
| apostrophe (') | shows missing letters or that something belongs to someone |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Question Marks and Exclamation Marks
A **question mark** (?) goes at the end of a sentence that asks something:
- What is your name?
- Where do you live?
- Can I have a biscuit?

An **exclamation mark** (!) shows strong feelings like surprise, excitement or an order:
- What a lovely day!
- Stop right there!
- I can't believe it!

### Commas in Lists
When we write a list of things, we put a **comma** between each item. We use **and** before the last item.

- I packed a sandwich, an apple, a drink and a biscuit.
- The flag was red, white and blue.
- We saw lions, elephants, giraffes and monkeys at the zoo.

### Apostrophes
Apostrophes have two jobs:

**1. Missing letters (contractions):**
- do not → don't
- I am → I'm
- cannot → can't
- it is → it's

**2. Belonging to someone (possession):**
- the dog's bone (the bone belongs to the dog)
- Sarah's coat (the coat belongs to Sarah)
- the teacher's desk (the desk belongs to the teacher)

## Did You Know? 🌟
The question mark may have come from the Latin word *quaestio* (meaning "question"). People think scholars used to write a **q** above an **o** at the end of questions, and over time it turned into the **?** shape we use today!
