# Grammar — Word Classes 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning about the different types of words we use in sentences. Every word has a job. Knowing about nouns, verbs, adjectives and adverbs helps us write more interesting sentences.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| noun | a word for a person, place or thing |
| verb | a doing or being word |
| adjective | a word that describes a noun |
| adverb | a word that describes how something is done |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Nouns
A **noun** is a naming word. It names a person, place or thing.

- **People:** teacher, girl, doctor, farmer
- **Places:** school, park, London, beach
- **Things:** book, table, cat, football

In the sentence "The **dog** chased the **ball** in the **garden**", the nouns are dog, ball and garden.

### Verbs
A **verb** is a doing word or a being word. It tells us what someone or something does.

- **Doing verbs:** run, jump, eat, write, sing
- **Being verbs:** is, am, are, was, were

In the sentence "The cat **sat** on the mat", the verb is sat.

### Adjectives
An **adjective** describes a noun. It tells us what something is like.

- The **big**, **brown** dog
- A **shiny**, **red** apple
- The **old**, **wooden** door

Adjectives answer: What does it look like? How does it feel? What colour is it?

### Adverbs
An **adverb** describes a verb. It tells us **how** something is done. Many adverbs end in **-ly**.

- She ran **quickly**.
- He spoke **quietly**.
- The snail moved **slowly**.
- The children sang **beautifully**.

## Did You Know? 🌟
The longest word in the English language that is also an adjective is **"pneumonoultramicroscopicsilicovolcanoconiosis"** — but don't worry, you won't need to learn that one until much, much later! Most adjectives are nice and short, like "big" or "red".
