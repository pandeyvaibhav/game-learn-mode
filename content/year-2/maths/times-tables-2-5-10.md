# Times Tables (2, 5 and 10) 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning the 2, 5 and 10 times tables. Multiplication means adding equal groups together. Knowing these times tables helps us count and solve problems much faster.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| multiply | add equal groups together |
| times | another word for multiply |
| equal groups | groups that all have the same number |
| skip counting | counting in jumps of the same number |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### The 2 Times Table
When we multiply by 2, we are counting in twos. Every answer is an even number.

| Calculation | Answer |
|-------------|--------|
| 1 × 2 | 2 |
| 2 × 2 | 4 |
| 3 × 2 | 6 |
| 4 × 2 | 8 |
| 5 × 2 | 10 |
| 6 × 2 | 12 |

Think of it as pairs: 3 × 2 means 3 pairs of socks = 6 socks.

### The 5 Times Table
When we multiply by 5, all the answers end in **0** or **5**.

| Calculation | Answer |
|-------------|--------|
| 1 × 5 | 5 |
| 2 × 5 | 10 |
| 3 × 5 | 15 |
| 4 × 5 | 20 |
| 5 × 5 | 25 |
| 6 × 5 | 30 |

Think of fingers: 4 × 5 means 4 hands = 20 fingers.

### The 10 Times Table
When we multiply by 10, we just put a **0** on the end!

| Calculation | Answer |
|-------------|--------|
| 1 × 10 | 10 |
| 2 × 10 | 20 |
| 3 × 10 | 30 |
| 4 × 10 | 40 |
| 5 × 10 | 50 |
| 6 × 10 | 60 |

Think of 10p coins: 3 × 10 means 3 coins of 10p = 30p.

## Did You Know? 🌟
If you hold up both hands and fold down one finger, you can use your fingers to work out the 9 times table! Try folding down your 3rd finger — you'll see 2 fingers on the left and 7 on the right, making 27 (3 × 9 = 27).
