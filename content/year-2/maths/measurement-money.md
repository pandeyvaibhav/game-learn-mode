# Measurement — Money 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning about coins and notes, how to add up amounts of money, and how to work out change. Understanding money helps us buy things and check we get the right amount back.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| coin | a piece of metal money (1p, 2p, 5p, 10p, 20p, 50p, £1, £2) |
| note | paper money (£5, £10, £20) |
| pence (p) | the smaller unit of British money |
| pound (£) | 100 pence make one pound |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Coins We Use
In the UK, we use these coins:

| Coin | Colour | Shape |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1p | copper | round |
| 2p | copper | round |
| 5p | silver | round |
| 10p | silver | round |
| 20p | silver | not round (heptagon) |
| 50p | silver | not round (heptagon) |
| £1 | gold & silver | round |
| £2 | gold & silver | round |

Remember: 100p = £1

### Adding Amounts
To add coins, start with the biggest coin and count on.

Example: 20p + 10p + 5p + 2p

- Start with 20p
- Add 10p → 30p
- Add 5p → 35p
- Add 2p → 37p

Total = **37p**

### Giving Change
Change is the money you get back when you pay more than something costs.

Example: A pencil costs 30p. You pay with 50p. How much change?

50p − 30p = **20p** change

You can count up from 30p to 50p:
30p → 40p → 50p = 20p change

## Did You Know? 🌟
The 20p and 50p coins have seven sides! This shape is called a **heptagon**. They were made this shape so people can tell them apart from round coins just by feeling them.
