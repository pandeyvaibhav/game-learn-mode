# Measurement — Money — Year 2 Maths

## Big Question
How do we recognise coins and notes, add amounts, and work out change?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn the values of UK **coins and notes**, how to **add** money amounts, and how to work out **change**.

## Key Maths Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| coin | a round piece of metal money (1p, 2p, 5p, 10p, 20p, 50p, £1, £2) |
| note | paper money (£5, £10, £20) |
| pence (p) | the smaller unit of money — 100p = £1 |
| pound (£) | the main unit of UK money |
| change | the money you get back when you pay too much |
| total | the amount when you add everything together |

## The Maths Explained

### UK Coins
1p, 2p, 5p, 10p, 20p, 50p, £1 (100p), £2 (200p)

### Adding Money
Add the coins together to find the total:
- 10p + 5p + 2p = **17p**
- 20p + 20p + 10p = **50p**
- 50p + 50p = **£1**

### Giving Change
If an apple costs 30p and you pay with 50p:
Change = 50p − 30p = **20p**

### Converting
£1 = 100p, so £1.50 = 150p

## Quick Quiz
1. How many pence in £1? → *100p*
2. What is 20p + 10p + 5p? → *35p*
3. An item costs 40p. You pay with 50p. What change? → *10p*
4. Name three UK coins. → *1p, 5p, 10p (or any three)*

## Maths in the Real World
You use money every day — buying snacks, saving in a piggy bank, or visiting a shop. Understanding coins and change helps you make sure you get the right amount back.

## Learning Checklist
- [ ] I can recognise and name all UK coins
- [ ] I can add amounts of money
- [ ] I can work out change from a given amount
