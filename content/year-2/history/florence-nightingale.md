# Florence Nightingale 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning about Florence Nightingale, a famous nurse who changed hospitals forever. She lived over 150 years ago and helped sick and wounded soldiers during the Crimean War. Her work made hospitals much cleaner and safer.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| nurse | a person who looks after sick and injured people |
| Crimean War | a war fought between 1853 and 1856, partly in a place called Crimea |
| hygiene | keeping things clean to stop germs spreading |
| ward | a room in a hospital with beds for patients |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Who Was Florence Nightingale?
Florence Nightingale was born on **12th May 1820** in Florence, Italy (that's how she got her name!). She grew up in a rich English family. Even as a child, she loved caring for sick animals and people.

At that time, most people thought nursing was not a proper job for a wealthy woman, but Florence was determined. She trained as a nurse against her family's wishes.

### The Crimean War
In 1854, Britain was fighting in the **Crimean War**. Soldiers were badly injured, and the hospital at **Scutari** (in Turkey) was filthy and overcrowded. Many soldiers were dying — not just from their wounds, but from **diseases** caused by dirt and germs.

Florence and **38 nurses** travelled to Scutari to help.

### What Did Florence Do?
When Florence arrived, she found terrible conditions:
- Dirty floors and bed sheets
- Rats and fleas everywhere
- Not enough medicine or bandages
- Blocked drains and no clean water

Florence made big changes:
- She **scrubbed** the floors and walls
- She washed sheets and bandages
- She gave soldiers proper food and clean water
- She made sure wards were well ventilated (fresh air)

### The Lady with the Lamp
Florence would walk through the wards at night carrying a **lamp**, checking on the soldiers. They called her **"The Lady with the Lamp"** and felt comforted by her care.

After the war, Florence used **statistics** (data and charts) to prove that better hygiene saved lives. She set up the first proper training school for nurses.

## Did You Know? 🌟
Every year on **12th May** (Florence's birthday), the world celebrates **International Nurses Day** in her honour. It is a day to say thank you to all the nurses who look after us!
