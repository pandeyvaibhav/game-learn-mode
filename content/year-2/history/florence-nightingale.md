# Florence Nightingale — Year 2 History

## Big Question
Who was Florence Nightingale and why is she important?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about the life of **Florence Nightingale**, how she changed **nursing** and **hospitals**, and why she is still remembered today.

## Key History Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| Florence Nightingale | a famous nurse who improved hospital care |
| Crimean War | a war fought in the 1850s, partly in what is now Ukraine |
| hygiene | keeping things clean to stop germs spreading |
| ward | a room in a hospital where patients are cared for |
| "Lady with the Lamp" | Florence's nickname because she checked on soldiers at night |
| reform | changing something to make it better |

## The History Explained

### Early Life
- Born in **1820** in Florence, Italy (that's where her name comes from!).
- Her family was wealthy, but she wanted to help sick people.
- She trained as a **nurse** even though her parents disapproved.

### The Crimean War (1853–1856)
- British soldiers were badly wounded and fell ill in dirty hospitals.
- Florence and **38 nurses** travelled to Scutari (in modern Turkey).
- The hospital was filthy — rats, no clean water, and overcrowded wards.

### What Florence Changed
1. **Cleaned the wards** — scrubbed floors, washed bedding.
2. **Improved food** — gave soldiers proper meals.
3. **Better hygiene** — fresh air, clean bandages.
4. The **death rate dropped** dramatically.

### The Lady with the Lamp
Florence walked the wards at night with a lamp, checking on patients. Soldiers called her the **"Lady with the Lamp"**.

### After the War
- She set up the **Nightingale Training School** for nurses in London.
- She used **statistics** (pie charts!) to prove that hygiene saved lives.
- She is known as the **founder of modern nursing**.

## Quick Quiz
1. When was Florence Nightingale born? → *1820*
2. Which war did she help in? → *The Crimean War*
3. Why was she called the "Lady with the Lamp"? → *She checked on soldiers at night carrying a lamp*
4. What did she improve in the hospitals? → *Hygiene / cleanliness*

## History in the Real World
Florence's work changed hospitals forever. Today nurses are trained to high standards and hygiene rules save millions of lives every year.
