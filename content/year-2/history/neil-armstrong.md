# Neil Armstrong — Year 2 History

## Big Question
Who was Neil Armstrong and why was walking on the Moon so important?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about **Neil Armstrong**, the first person to walk on the **Moon**, the **Apollo 11** mission, and why space exploration matters.

## Key History Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| Neil Armstrong | the first person to walk on the Moon |
| Apollo 11 | the name of the space mission to the Moon |
| astronaut | a person who travels into space |
| Moon landing | when the spacecraft touched down on the Moon |
| NASA | the American space agency |
| orbit | to travel around something in a curved path |

## The History Explained

### Who Was Neil Armstrong?
- Born on **5 August 1930** in Ohio, USA.
- Loved **aeroplanes** from a young age.
- Became a **pilot** and then an **astronaut**.

### The Apollo 11 Mission (1969)
- **Date**: 20 July 1969
- **Crew**: Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, and Michael Collins.
- The rocket **Saturn V** launched from Florida, USA.
- Armstrong and Aldrin landed on the Moon in the lunar module called **Eagle**.
- Collins stayed in orbit around the Moon.

### One Small Step
When Armstrong stepped onto the Moon he said:
> *"That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."*

He and Aldrin spent about **2 hours** walking on the Moon, collecting rocks and planting the American flag.

### Why It Matters
- It showed that humans could travel beyond Earth.
- Scientists learned about the Moon from the rocks brought back.
- It inspired millions of people around the world.

## Quick Quiz
1. In which year did Neil Armstrong walk on the Moon? → *1969*
2. What was the name of the mission? → *Apollo 11*
3. Name the two astronauts who walked on the Moon. → *Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin*
4. What did Armstrong say when he stepped on the Moon? → *"That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."*

## History in the Real World
Space exploration continues today with the International Space Station and plans to return to the Moon. Armstrong's bravery opened the door to a future in space.
