# Neil Armstrong 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning about Neil Armstrong, the first person to walk on the Moon. He went to the Moon in 1969 as part of the Apollo 11 mission. His achievement is one of the most important events in history.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| astronaut | a person who travels into space |
| Apollo 11 | the name of the space mission that went to the Moon |
| NASA | the American space agency that organised the mission |
| lunar module | the small spacecraft that landed on the Moon |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Who Was Neil Armstrong?
Neil Armstrong was born on **5th August 1930** in Ohio, USA. He loved aeroplanes from a young age and learned to fly before he could even drive a car! He became a pilot and then a **NASA astronaut**.

### The Apollo 11 Mission
On **16th July 1969**, Apollo 11 launched from Florida, USA. Three astronauts were on board:

- **Neil Armstrong** — Mission Commander
- **Buzz Aldrin** — Lunar Module Pilot
- **Michael Collins** — Command Module Pilot

The journey to the Moon took about **three days**.

### Walking on the Moon
On **20th July 1969**, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin climbed into the lunar module (called **Eagle**) and landed on the Moon. Michael Collins stayed in the spacecraft orbiting above.

Neil Armstrong was the **first person** to step onto the Moon's surface. He said the famous words:

> "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."

He and Buzz Aldrin spent about **two and a half hours** walking on the Moon. They:
- Collected Moon rocks and soil
- Planted an American flag
- Took photographs
- Set up science experiments

### Coming Home
The astronauts returned safely to Earth on **24th July 1969**, splashing down in the Pacific Ocean. The whole world watched and celebrated.

## Did You Know? 🌟
The astronauts' footprints are **still on the Moon** today! Because there is no wind or rain on the Moon, the footprints will stay there for millions of years.
