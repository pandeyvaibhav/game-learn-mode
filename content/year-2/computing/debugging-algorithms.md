# Debugging Algorithms 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning about algorithms and how to find and fix mistakes in them. An algorithm is a set of step-by-step instructions. When something goes wrong, we need to **debug** it — that means finding the mistake and fixing it.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| algorithm | a set of step-by-step instructions to do something |
| debug | finding and fixing a mistake in an algorithm or program |
| bug | a mistake or error |
| sequence | the order that instructions happen in |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### What Is an Algorithm?
An algorithm is like a recipe — it tells you what to do, one step at a time, in the right order.

Here is an algorithm for making a jam sandwich:
1. Get two slices of bread
2. Pick up the knife
3. Spread butter on both slices
4. Spread jam on one slice
5. Put the two slices together

If we do the steps **in the wrong order**, the sandwich won't be right. The order (sequence) matters!

### What Is a Bug?
A **bug** is a mistake in an algorithm. It might be:
- A **missing step** (you forgot something)
- A **wrong step** (you did the wrong thing)
- Steps in the **wrong order**

### Debugging
**Debugging** means finding the bug and fixing it. To debug:

1. **Read** through the algorithm carefully
2. **Check** each step — does it make sense?
3. **Find** the mistake
4. **Fix** it!

Example — Can you spot the bug?

Algorithm for brushing teeth:
1. Pick up toothbrush
2. Brush your teeth
3. Put toothpaste on the brush
4. Rinse your mouth

The bug is that steps 2 and 3 are in the **wrong order**! You need to put toothpaste on **before** you brush.

Fixed algorithm:
1. Pick up toothbrush
2. Put toothpaste on the brush
3. Brush your teeth
4. Rinse your mouth

## Did You Know? 🌟
The word **"bug"** in computing comes from 1947, when a real moth got stuck inside a computer at Harvard University! The engineers taped the moth into their logbook and wrote "First actual case of bug being found." Since then, we call computer mistakes "bugs"!
