# Online Safety — Year 2 Computing

## Big Question
How can we stay safe and be kind when we use the internet?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn rules for staying safe online: keeping **personal information** private, telling a **trusted adult** if something worries you, and being **kind** to others online.

## Key Computing Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| personal information | details about you — your name, address, school, password |
| trusted adult | a grown-up you can talk to if you are worried (parent, teacher) |
| password | a secret word that keeps your accounts safe |
| internet | a network that connects computers around the world |
| screenshot | a picture of what is on your screen |
| cyberbullying | being mean to someone online |

## The Computing Explained

### Personal Information — Keep It Private!
**Never share** these online:
- Your **full name**
- Your **address** or **school name**
- Your **phone number**
- Your **password**

If a website or game asks for personal information, always ask a trusted adult first.

### Trusted Adults
If anything online makes you feel **scared**, **sad**, or **confused**:
1. **Stop** — don't keep looking.
2. **Tell** a trusted adult straight away.
3. They can help you deal with it.

Trusted adults include: parents, carers, teachers, and other family members.

### Being Kind Online
The internet is used by **real people** with **real feelings**.
- Use **kind words** — treat people as you would face-to-face.
- **Don't** send mean messages or leave hurtful comments.
- If someone is being unkind to you, **tell a trusted adult**.

### Password Safety
- Keep your password **secret** — don't share it with friends.
- Use a password that is **hard to guess**.
- Never use your name or birthday.

## Quick Quiz
1. Name one thing you should never share online. → *Your address (or password, phone number, school name)*
2. What should you do if something online worries you? → *Tell a trusted adult*
3. Why should you be kind online? → *Because real people with real feelings are on the other side*
4. Should you share your password with friends? → *No*

## Computing in the Real World
Online safety rules help everyone — children and adults — stay protected. Companies use passwords and privacy settings to keep data safe.

## Learning Checklist
- [ ] I know what personal information is and why I must keep it private
- [ ] I know to tell a trusted adult if something online worries me
- [ ] I understand why I should be kind online
