# Networks & Cybersafety — Year 6 Computing

## Big Question
How does the internet work and how can we stay safe online?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about **computer networks**, how the **internet** works, and essential **online safety** practices including **passwords** and **privacy**.

## Key Computing Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| network | two or more computers connected to share data |
| internet | a global network of networks |
| router | a device that directs data between networks |
| IP address | a unique number that identifies a device on a network |
| password | a secret code used to protect accounts |
| privacy | controlling who can see your personal information |
| phishing | a trick to steal personal information by pretending to be trustworthy |

## The Computing Explained

### What is a Network?
- A **LAN** (Local Area Network) connects devices in one building (e.g. school).
- A **WAN** (Wide Area Network) connects devices across large distances.
- The **internet** is the biggest WAN — it connects billions of devices worldwide.

### How Data Travels
1. You request a webpage (e.g. type a URL).
2. The request travels as **packets** through routers.
3. The **server** sends the webpage data back in packets.
4. Your browser reassembles the packets into the page you see.

### Online Safety Rules
- **Never share** your full name, address, school, or phone number online.
- **Tell a trusted adult** if something online makes you uncomfortable.
- **Think before you post** — it may stay online forever.
- **Be kind** — treat others online as you would face to face.

### Strong Passwords
- Use a **mix** of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
- Make it **at least 8 characters** long.
- **Don't reuse** the same password for different sites.
- Never share your password with friends.

### Spotting Phishing
- Check the sender's email address carefully.
- Look for spelling mistakes and urgent language.
- Never click suspicious links.
- If in doubt, ask a trusted adult.

## Computing in the Real World
Networks power everything from school Wi-Fi to global banking. Cybersafety skills protect you now and throughout your life as technology grows.
