# Networks and Cyber Safety 🎯

## What We're Learning
Every time you go online, you're using a computer network. We'll learn how the internet works, what keeps us safe online, and why managing your digital footprint matters — skills that are essential for life in the digital age.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| network | Two or more devices connected together to share data |
| internet | A global network connecting billions of devices worldwide |
| IP address | A unique number that identifies a device on a network (like a postal address) |
| digital footprint | The trail of data you leave behind when you use the internet |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### How Networks Work
A **network** connects devices so they can share data, files, and resources. Your school network connects computers to a shared printer and the internet.

The **internet** is a worldwide network of networks. When you visit a website:
1. Your device sends a request through your **router** to your Internet Service Provider (ISP).
2. The request travels across the internet to a **server** (a powerful computer that stores the website).
3. The server sends the data back to your device.

Every device on a network has an **IP address** — a unique number like 192.168.1.25 — so data reaches the right place, like a postal address for computers.

### Cyber Security
Staying safe online means protecting your information and devices:
- **Strong passwords:** Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Never share them.
- **Phishing:** Fake emails or messages that try to trick you into giving away personal information. Always check the sender and don't click suspicious links.
- **Software updates:** Keep devices updated to fix security weaknesses.
- **Personal information:** Never share your full name, school, address, or photos with strangers online.

### Your Digital Footprint
Everything you do online leaves a trace — posts, searches, likes, comments. This is your **digital footprint**. It can be seen by others, sometimes years later, and may affect future opportunities like jobs or university applications.

Think before you post: Would you be happy for a teacher or future employer to see this?

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** What is a computer network?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>A computer network is two or more devices connected together so they can share data, files, and resources like printers or internet access.</details>

**Question 2:** What is an IP address and why is it important?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>An IP address is a unique number assigned to each device on a network. It's important because it ensures data is sent to and from the correct device, like a postal address for computers.</details>

**Question 3:** What is phishing and how can you spot it?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Phishing is when someone sends fake emails or messages pretending to be a trusted organisation to trick you into sharing personal information. Warning signs include: urgent language, spelling mistakes, suspicious sender addresses, and links to unfamiliar websites.</details>

**Question 4:** Give three tips for creating a strong password.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>1. Use a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. 2. Make it at least 8 characters long. 3. Don't use personal information like your name or birthday. Use a different password for each account.</details>

**Question 5:** What is a digital footprint and why should you care about it?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>A digital footprint is the trail of data you leave when using the internet — everything from posts and comments to search history. You should care because it can be seen by others, may be permanent, and could affect your reputation or future opportunities.</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
The internet was originally created in the late 1960s as a US military project called **ARPANET**. The first ever message sent across it (in 1969) was supposed to be "LOGIN", but the system crashed after just two letters — so the first internet message was actually "LO"!
