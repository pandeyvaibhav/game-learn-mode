# Ratio & Proportion — Year 6 Maths

## Big Question
How do we compare amounts using ratio and proportion?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn what **ratio** and **proportion** mean, how to **simplify** ratios, and how to **scale** amounts up or down.

## Key Maths Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| ratio | a way of comparing two or more amounts (e.g. 3:1) |
| proportion | a part compared to the whole (e.g. 1 in every 4) |
| simplify | make a ratio as small as possible (like simplifying fractions) |
| scale | to make something bigger or smaller by the same factor |
| equivalent ratio | ratios that represent the same comparison (e.g. 2:4 = 1:2) |

## The Maths Explained

### Ratio
A ratio compares parts to parts:
- 3 red and 1 blue → ratio 3:1
- 2 boys and 5 girls → ratio 2:5

### Simplifying Ratios
Divide both sides by the highest common factor:
- 6:4 → ÷2 → **3:2**
- 10:15 → ÷5 → **2:3**

### Proportion
Proportion compares a part to the whole:
- 3 red out of 4 total → **¾** or **75%** are red.

### Scaling with Ratio
A recipe serves 4 people using 200 g flour. For 8 people (×2):
- 200 g × 2 = **400 g** flour.

For 2 people (÷2):
- 200 g ÷ 2 = **100 g** flour.

### Solving Ratio Problems
If the ratio of cats to dogs is 3:2 and there are 15 cats:
- 3 parts = 15, so 1 part = 5
- Dogs = 2 × 5 = **10**

## Quick Quiz
1. Simplify 8:12 → *2:3*
2. If the ratio is 1:4 and there are 5 total, how many in the smaller group? → *1*
3. A recipe needs 300 g for 6 people. How much for 3? → *150 g*
4. Ratio 2:3, total 25. How many in the larger group? → *15*

## Maths in the Real World
Chefs scale recipes, map makers use scale ratios, and builders use proportion to mix concrete. Ratio appears in art, music, and finance.
