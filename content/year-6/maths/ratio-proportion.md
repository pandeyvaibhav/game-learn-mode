# Ratio and Proportion 🎯

## What We're Learning
Ratio and proportion help us compare quantities and scale things up or down. Whether you're following a recipe for more people or reading a map, you're using these skills every day.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| ratio | A way of comparing two or more quantities (e.g. 3:1) |
| proportion | When two ratios are equal, or a part compared to the whole |
| scale factor | The number you multiply by to enlarge or reduce something |
| simplify | Reducing a ratio to its smallest whole numbers |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Understanding Ratios
A ratio compares quantities using a colon. If a class has 12 boys and 18 girls, the ratio of boys to girls is **12:18**. We simplify by dividing both sides by the highest common factor (6), giving **2:3**. This means for every 2 boys, there are 3 girls.

Order matters! The ratio of boys to girls (2:3) is different from girls to boys (3:2).

### Proportion Problems
Proportion means quantities change at the same rate. If 3 pens cost £1.50, how much do 7 pens cost?

1. Find the cost of 1 pen: £1.50 ÷ 3 = £0.50
2. Multiply by 7: £0.50 × 7 = **£3.50**

This is called the **unitary method** — find the value of one unit first.

### Scale Factors
A scale factor tells us how many times bigger or smaller something becomes. If a map scale is 1:50,000, then 1 cm on the map represents 50,000 cm (500 m) in real life.

To enlarge a shape by scale factor 3, multiply every length by 3. A rectangle 2 cm × 4 cm becomes 6 cm × 12 cm.

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** Simplify the ratio 20:35.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Divide both by 5: 4:7</details>

**Question 2:** Purple paint is mixed using 2 parts red to 5 parts blue. How much blue is needed for 6 litres of red?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>The ratio is 2:5. Scale factor = 6 ÷ 2 = 3. Blue = 5 × 3 = 15 litres.</details>

**Question 3:** If 4 tickets cost £30, how much do 10 tickets cost?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>1 ticket = £30 ÷ 4 = £7.50. 10 tickets = £7.50 × 10 = £75.</details>

**Question 4:** A model car is made with scale 1:20. The real car is 4 m long. How long is the model?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>4 m ÷ 20 = 0.2 m = 20 cm</details>

**Question 5:** In a bag of sweets, the ratio of red to green to yellow is 2:3:5. If there are 60 sweets, how many are green?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Total parts = 2 + 3 + 5 = 10. Each part = 60 ÷ 10 = 6. Green = 3 × 6 = 18 sweets.</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
The golden ratio (approximately 1:1.618) appears throughout nature — in the spiral of seashells, the arrangement of sunflower seeds, and even in the proportions of the human face!
