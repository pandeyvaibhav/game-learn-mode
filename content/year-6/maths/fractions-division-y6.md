# Fractions and Division 🎯

## What We're Learning
In Year 6 we take our fraction skills further by multiplying and dividing fractions. These skills help us solve real-world problems like sharing recipes, measuring materials, and working with data.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| numerator | The top number of a fraction — how many parts we have |
| denominator | The bottom number of a fraction — how many equal parts in total |
| mixed number | A number with a whole part and a fraction part (e.g. 2¾) |
| improper fraction | A fraction where the numerator is larger than the denominator (e.g. 11/4) |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Multiplying Fractions
To multiply fractions, multiply the numerators together and multiply the denominators together. Then simplify if you can.

**Example:** ²⁄₃ × ⁴⁄₅ = (2 × 4) / (3 × 5) = **⁸⁄₁₅**

To multiply a fraction by a whole number, write the whole number as a fraction over 1:
**Example:** ³⁄₄ × 6 = ³⁄₄ × ⁶⁄₁ = ¹⁸⁄₄ = 4½

### Dividing Fractions by Whole Numbers
To divide a fraction by a whole number, multiply the denominator by that number.

**Example:** ³⁄₅ ÷ 2 = ³⁄₁₀

Think about it: if you have ³⁄₅ of a pizza and share it between 2 people, each person gets ³⁄₁₀ of the whole pizza.

### Mixed Numbers
Mixed numbers combine whole numbers and fractions. To use them in calculations, first convert to improper fractions.

**Example:** 2¹⁄₃ as an improper fraction: (2 × 3 + 1) / 3 = **⁷⁄₃**

To convert back: ⁷⁄₃ = 7 ÷ 3 = 2 remainder 1, so **2¹⁄₃**.

## Did You Know? 🌟
Ancient Egyptians only used unit fractions (fractions with 1 on top, like ½, ¹⁄₃, ¹⁄₇). They would write ²⁄₅ as ¹⁄₃ + ¹⁄₁₅. They carved these fractions into stone over 3,500 years ago!
