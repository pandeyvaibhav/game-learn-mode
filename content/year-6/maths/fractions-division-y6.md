# Fractions & Division — Year 6 Maths

## Big Question
How do we multiply and divide fractions?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn to **multiply** and **divide** fractions, work with **mixed numbers**, and **simplify** answers.

## Key Maths Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| numerator | the top number of a fraction |
| denominator | the bottom number of a fraction |
| mixed number | a whole number and a fraction together (e.g. 2¾) |
| improper fraction | a fraction where the numerator is bigger than the denominator (e.g. 11/4) |
| reciprocal | a fraction flipped upside down (e.g. reciprocal of ¾ is 4/3) |
| simplify | divide numerator and denominator by their highest common factor |

## The Maths Explained

### Multiplying Fractions
Multiply numerators together, multiply denominators together:
- ½ × ¾ = (1×3)/(2×4) = **3/8**
- ⅔ × ⅘ = (2×4)/(3×5) = **8/15**

### Dividing Fractions
**Keep, Change, Flip**: Keep the first fraction, change ÷ to ×, flip the second:
- ½ ÷ ¼ = ½ × 4/1 = **4/2 = 2**
- ¾ ÷ ½ = ¾ × 2/1 = **6/4 = 3/2 = 1½**

### Mixed Numbers
Convert to improper fractions first:
- 1½ = 3/2
- 2¼ = 9/4

Then multiply or divide as normal.

### Simplifying
Always simplify your answer:
- 6/8 → ÷2 → **¾**
- 4/10 → ÷2 → **⅖**

## Quick Quiz
1. ½ × ⅓ = ? → *1/6*
2. ¾ ÷ ½ = ? → *3/2 or 1½*
3. Convert 2⅓ to an improper fraction. → *7/3*
4. Simplify 6/9. → *2/3*

## Maths in the Real World
Fractions are used in cooking (halving recipes), construction (measuring materials), and finance (calculating discounts and interest).

## Learning Checklist
- [ ] I can multiply fractions
- [ ] I can divide fractions using Keep, Change, Flip
- [ ] I can convert mixed numbers and simplify answers
