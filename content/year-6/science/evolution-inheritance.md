# Evolution and Inheritance 🎯

## What We're Learning
Living things have changed over millions of years through evolution. We'll explore how fossils tell us about the past, how offspring inherit characteristics from their parents, and how species adapt to survive in their environments.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| evolution | The gradual change in living things over very long periods of time |
| inheritance | The passing of characteristics from parents to their offspring |
| adaptation | A feature that helps a living thing survive in its environment |
| natural selection | The process where organisms better suited to their environment survive and reproduce |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Fossils and Evidence for Evolution
Fossils are the preserved remains or traces of living things from millions of years ago. They form when organisms are buried in sediment that slowly turns to rock. Fossils show us that life on Earth has changed enormously — for example, we know dinosaurs once roamed the Earth and that horses were once the size of dogs.

Mary Anning, a famous British fossil hunter from Dorset, discovered important fossils in the early 1800s that helped scientists understand prehistoric life.

### Inheritance and Variation
Children inherit characteristics from their parents — eye colour, hair type, height potential. This is why family members often look alike. However, offspring are never exact copies. **Variation** means individuals of the same species have differences. Some variation is inherited (eye colour) and some is caused by the environment (a scar, or being very fit from exercise).

### Adaptation and Natural Selection
Over time, living things develop features that help them survive. Polar bears have thick white fur for insulation and camouflage. Cacti have spines instead of leaves to reduce water loss.

**Natural selection** explains how this happens: individuals with helpful variations are more likely to survive, reproduce, and pass those traits on. Over many generations, the whole species changes. Charles Darwin developed this theory after studying finches in the Galápagos Islands.

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** What are fossils and how do they form?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Fossils are preserved remains or traces of organisms from long ago. They form when dead organisms are buried in sediment, which hardens into rock over millions of years, preserving the shape of the organism.</details>

**Question 2:** Give two examples of inherited characteristics and one that is caused by the environment.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Inherited: eye colour, natural hair colour. Environmental: a scar, or speaking a particular language.</details>

**Question 3:** How is a cactus adapted to survive in the desert?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>It has spines instead of leaves to reduce water loss, thick stems to store water, and long roots to reach water deep underground.</details>

**Question 4:** Explain natural selection in your own words.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Living things with features that help them survive are more likely to live long enough to have babies. Those babies inherit the helpful features, so over time, more of the species have those useful traits.</details>

**Question 5:** Why are offspring of the same parents not identical to each other?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Each offspring receives a different combination of genes from both parents, creating natural variation. This is why siblings can have different eye colours, hair types, or heights.</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
The peppered moth is a famous example of natural selection in action! Before the Industrial Revolution, most peppered moths were pale. When pollution darkened the trees, dark-coloured moths survived better because birds couldn't spot them — so the population shifted to mostly dark moths.
