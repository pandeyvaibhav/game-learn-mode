# Evolution & Inheritance — Year 6 Science

## Big Question
How have living things changed over time and why do offspring look like their parents?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about **evolution**, **natural selection**, **inheritance**, and how **fossils** provide evidence of change over millions of years.

## Key Science Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| evolution | gradual change in living things over many generations |
| natural selection | organisms best suited to their environment are more likely to survive and reproduce |
| adaptation | a feature that helps an organism survive in its habitat |
| inheritance | characteristics passed from parents to offspring |
| fossil | preserved remains or traces of organisms from long ago |
| variation | differences between individuals of the same species |

## The Science Explained

### Inheritance
- Children inherit characteristics from **both parents** (e.g. eye colour, hair type).
- This is why family members often look alike.
- Not everything is inherited — a scar is acquired, not inherited.

### Variation
- Even within the same species, individuals are **not identical** (except identical twins).
- Variation means some individuals may be better suited to their environment.

### Natural Selection
1. There is **variation** within a species.
2. Some variations give an **advantage** (e.g. longer beak reaches food).
3. Those individuals are more likely to **survive and reproduce**.
4. The helpful trait is **passed on** to offspring.
5. Over many generations, the species **changes** — this is evolution.

### Fossils as Evidence
- Fossils show what organisms looked like millions of years ago.
- By comparing fossils with living things, scientists can trace how species changed.
- Some fossils show species that are now **extinct**.

### Famous Example
Darwin's finches on the Galápagos Islands had different beak shapes suited to different foods — evidence of adaptation and natural selection.

## Quick Quiz
1. What is natural selection? → *Organisms best suited to their environment survive and reproduce*
2. What is inheritance? → *Characteristics passed from parents to offspring*
3. How do fossils help us understand evolution? → *They show what organisms looked like long ago so we can trace changes*
4. What is variation? → *Differences between individuals of the same species*

## Science in the Real World
Understanding evolution helps doctors tackle antibiotic-resistant bacteria and helps conservationists protect endangered species.
