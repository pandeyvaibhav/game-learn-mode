# Living Things and Classification 🎯

## What We're Learning
Scientists have classified millions of living things into groups based on their characteristics. We'll learn about the classification system created by Carl Linnaeus, explore the kingdoms of life, and discover the hidden world of microorganisms.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| classification | Sorting living things into groups based on shared features |
| kingdom | One of the broadest groups living things are sorted into |
| microorganism | A living thing too small to see without a microscope |
| Linnaeus | The Swedish scientist who created the modern classification system |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### The Linnaeus Classification System
Carl Linnaeus (1707–1778) created a system to organise all living things into groups. His system starts broad and gets more specific:

**Kingdom → Phylum → Class → Order → Family → Genus → Species**

A helpful way to remember: **K**ing **P**hilip **C**ame **O**ver **F**or **G**ood **S**paghetti!

Every species has a two-part Latin name (genus + species). Humans are *Homo sapiens*. This means scientists worldwide can identify the same organism, no matter what language they speak.

### The Five Kingdoms
Living things are sorted into broad groups:
1. **Animals** — multicellular, move, eat other organisms
2. **Plants** — multicellular, make food by photosynthesis
3. **Fungi** — mushrooms, moulds, yeast — absorb nutrients from dead matter
4. **Protists** — mostly single-celled organisms like amoeba
5. **Bacteria** — tiny single-celled organisms found everywhere

### Microorganisms
Microorganisms (microbes) include bacteria, some fungi, and protists. They are everywhere — in soil, water, air, and inside us! Some are helpful (gut bacteria help digest food, yeast makes bread rise), while others cause disease (harmful bacteria can cause food poisoning).

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** Who was Carl Linnaeus and what did he create?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Carl Linnaeus was a Swedish scientist who created the modern classification system for sorting all living things into groups based on shared characteristics.</details>

**Question 2:** Put these in order from broadest to most specific: species, kingdom, class, genus.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Kingdom → Class → Genus → Species</details>

**Question 3:** Name the five kingdoms of living things.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Animals, Plants, Fungi, Protists, Bacteria</details>

**Question 4:** Give one example of a helpful microorganism and one that is harmful.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Helpful: yeast (used in baking bread). Harmful: Salmonella bacteria (causes food poisoning).</details>

**Question 5:** Why do scientists use Latin names for species?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>So that scientists around the world can identify the same organism using one universal name, regardless of what language they speak. A robin in English might have a different name in French, but *Erithacus rubecula* is the same everywhere.</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
There are more bacteria in your gut than there are cells in your whole body! These "friendly" bacteria help you digest food and keep you healthy. Scientists call them your **microbiome**.
