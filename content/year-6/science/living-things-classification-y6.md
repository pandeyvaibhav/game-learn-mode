# Living Things Classification — Year 6 Science

## Big Question
How do scientists sort living things into groups and why is classification useful?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about **classification systems**, the five **kingdoms**, and how to classify **vertebrates**, **invertebrates**, and **microorganisms**.

## Key Science Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| classification | sorting living things into groups based on characteristics |
| kingdom | the largest group in classification (animals, plants, fungi, protists, bacteria) |
| vertebrate | an animal with a backbone |
| invertebrate | an animal without a backbone |
| microorganism | a living thing too small to see without a microscope |
| species | a group of organisms that can reproduce together |

## The Science Explained

### The Five Kingdoms
| Kingdom | Examples | Key features |
|---|---|---|
| Animals | Humans, insects, fish | Move, eat other organisms |
| Plants | Trees, flowers, ferns | Make food by photosynthesis |
| Fungi | Mushrooms, mould, yeast | Decompose organic matter |
| Protists | Amoeba, algae | Mostly single-celled |
| Bacteria | E. coli, streptococcus | Single-celled, no nucleus |

### Vertebrate Groups
| Group | Examples | Features |
|---|---|---|
| Mammals | Dog, whale, human | Warm-blooded, fur/hair, live young |
| Birds | Robin, eagle, penguin | Warm-blooded, feathers, lay eggs |
| Reptiles | Snake, lizard, turtle | Cold-blooded, scales, lay eggs |
| Amphibians | Frog, newt, salamander | Cold-blooded, moist skin, metamorphosis |
| Fish | Salmon, shark, clownfish | Cold-blooded, scales, gills |

### Invertebrates
Over 95% of animals are invertebrates. Examples: insects, spiders, worms, jellyfish, snails.

### Microorganisms
- **Bacteria** — some helpful (yoghurt), some harmful (infections).
- **Viruses** — cause colds, flu; need a host to reproduce.
- **Fungi** — yeast makes bread rise; mould decomposes matter.

## Quick Quiz
1. What are the five kingdoms? → *Animals, plants, fungi, protists, bacteria*
2. What is a vertebrate? → *An animal with a backbone*
3. Name the five vertebrate groups. → *Mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, fish*
4. What is a microorganism? → *A living thing too small to see without a microscope*

## Science in the Real World
Classification helps scientists discover new species, track disease, and understand how ecosystems work.
