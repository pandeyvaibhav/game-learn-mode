# Light 🎯

## What We're Learning
Light is a form of energy that allows us to see. In Year 6, we explore how light travels in straight lines, how shadows are formed, and how light behaves when it hits different surfaces or passes through different materials.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| light source | An object that produces its own light (e.g. the Sun, a torch) |
| reflection | When light bounces off a surface |
| refraction | When light changes direction as it passes from one material to another |
| shadow | A dark area formed when an opaque object blocks light |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Light Travels in Straight Lines
Light always travels in straight lines. We can prove this because:
- Shadows have sharp edges
- We can't see around corners
- A beam of light through dust shows a straight line

We see objects because light from a source travels to the object, bounces off it, and enters our eyes. If there's no light, we can't see anything.

### Shadows
Shadows form when an **opaque** object blocks light. The shadow is the same shape as the object but can change size depending on the distance between the light source and the object.

- Move the object **closer** to the light → bigger shadow
- Move the object **further** from the light → smaller shadow

**Transparent** objects (glass) let light through — no shadow. **Translucent** objects (frosted glass) let some light through — faint shadow.

### Reflection and Refraction
**Reflection** happens when light bounces off a surface. Smooth, shiny surfaces (mirrors, still water) create clear reflections. The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection.

**Refraction** occurs when light passes from one material to another (e.g. air to water) and changes speed, causing it to bend. This is why a straw in a glass of water looks bent!

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** Why can we see the Moon even though it is not a light source?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>The Moon reflects light from the Sun. The sunlight bounces off the Moon's surface and travels to our eyes.</details>

**Question 2:** How do we know that light travels in straight lines?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Shadows have sharp edges, we cannot see around corners, and beams of light (e.g. through a gap in curtains) appear as straight lines.</details>

**Question 3:** What happens to a shadow when the object is moved closer to the light source?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>The shadow gets bigger because the object blocks a larger portion of the light.</details>

**Question 4:** Explain why a pencil looks bent when placed in a glass of water.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>This is caused by refraction. Light changes speed and direction as it passes from water to air, making the pencil appear bent at the water's surface.</details>

**Question 5:** What is the difference between transparent, translucent, and opaque?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Transparent materials let all light through (clear glass). Translucent materials let some light through (frosted glass). Opaque materials block all light (wood, metal) and create shadows.</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
Light is the fastest thing in the universe, travelling at about 300,000 kilometres per second! It takes light from the Sun about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth — so when you look at the Sun, you're actually seeing it as it was 8 minutes ago.
