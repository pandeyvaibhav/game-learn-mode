# Light — Year 6 Science

## Big Question
How does light travel and what happens when it hits different surfaces?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn that light travels in **straight lines**, how **reflection** and **refraction** work, how **shadows** form, and about the visible **spectrum**.

## Key Science Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| light source | something that produces its own light (e.g. the Sun, a torch) |
| reflection | light bouncing off a surface |
| refraction | light bending as it passes from one material to another |
| shadow | a dark area formed when an object blocks light |
| spectrum | the band of colours that make up white light |
| opaque | does not let light through |

## The Science Explained

### Light Travels in Straight Lines
- We can see objects because light from a source travels to the object, reflects off it, and enters our eyes.
- Shadows prove light travels straight — the shadow has the same shape as the object blocking the light.

### Reflection
- Light bounces off smooth, shiny surfaces (mirrors, still water).
- **Angle of incidence = angle of reflection.**

### Refraction
- Light bends when it moves between materials of different density (e.g. air → water).
- This is why a straw in water looks bent.

### Shadows
- Formed when an **opaque** object blocks light.
- Shadow size changes with the distance between the object and the light source.
- Closer to the light → bigger shadow; further away → smaller shadow.

### The Spectrum
White light splits into **red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, violet** (a rainbow) when passed through a prism.

## Quick Quiz
1. Why can we see objects? → *Light reflects off them into our eyes*
2. What is refraction? → *Light bending as it passes between materials*
3. Why does a shadow have the same shape as the object? → *Because light travels in straight lines*
4. What colours make up the visible spectrum? → *Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, violet*

## Science in the Real World
Understanding light is essential for cameras, glasses, telescopes, fibre-optic internet, and laser technology.
