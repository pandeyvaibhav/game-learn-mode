# Electricity and Circuits 🎯

## What We're Learning
In Year 6 we build on what we know about circuits by learning standard circuit symbols, investigating how voltage affects components, and understanding why changing parts of a circuit changes how it works.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| circuit | A complete loop that electricity flows around |
| voltage | The push that drives electricity around a circuit (measured in volts) |
| component | A part of a circuit, such as a bulb, buzzer, switch, or motor |
| circuit diagram | A drawing using standard symbols to show how a circuit is connected |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Circuit Symbols
Scientists use standard symbols so that anyone can understand a circuit diagram:

- **Cell (battery):** Two lines (one long, one short)
- **Wire:** A straight line
- **Bulb:** A circle with a cross inside
- **Switch (open):** A gap in the line
- **Switch (closed):** A connected line
- **Motor:** An M in a circle
- **Buzzer:** A semicircle with two lines

Using symbols makes diagrams quick to draw and universally understood.

### Voltage and Brightness
**Voltage** is the "push" that makes electricity flow. A single cell might be 1.5V. Adding more cells in a series circuit increases the voltage, making bulbs brighter and motors spin faster.

However, adding more bulbs to the **same** series circuit means each bulb gets less voltage, so they become dimmer. The total voltage is shared between the components.

### Investigating Circuits
When you change one variable in a circuit, you can observe the effect:
- **More cells** → more voltage → brighter bulb
- **More bulbs in series** → same voltage shared → dimmer bulbs
- **A break in the circuit** → no current flows → nothing works
- **A switch** → controls the flow — open means off, closed means on

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** What happens to the brightness of a bulb if you add another cell to a series circuit?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>The bulb gets brighter because there is more voltage (a greater push) to drive the electricity around the circuit.</details>

**Question 2:** Why does a bulb go dim if you add a second bulb to the same series circuit?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>The voltage is shared between the two bulbs, so each one gets less electrical push than before.</details>

**Question 3:** Draw (or describe) the standard symbol for a bulb.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>A circle with a cross (×) inside it.</details>

**Question 4:** Why won't a circuit work if there is a gap in it?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Electricity needs a complete loop to flow. A gap breaks the circuit, so current cannot travel around it and the components will not work.</details>

**Question 5:** A circuit has a 3V battery and one bulb. Another circuit has a 3V battery and three bulbs in series. Which circuit has the brightest bulbs?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>The circuit with one bulb. It receives all 3V, while in the other circuit the 3V is shared among three bulbs (1V each), making them much dimmer.</details>
