# Electricity & Circuits — Year 6 Science

## Big Question
How do electrical circuits work and what affects the brightness of a bulb?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about **circuit diagrams**, **circuit symbols**, **voltage**, and how changing **components** affects a circuit.

## Key Science Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| circuit | a complete loop that electricity flows around |
| voltage | the push that drives electricity around a circuit (measured in volts) |
| component | a part of a circuit (e.g. bulb, switch, motor, buzzer) |
| cell | a single unit that provides voltage (often called a battery) |
| battery | two or more cells joined together |
| series circuit | a circuit with one loop — all components share the same path |

## The Science Explained

### Circuit Symbols
| Component | Symbol description |
|---|---|
| Cell | Long and short parallel lines |
| Battery | Multiple cells joined |
| Bulb (lamp) | Circle with a cross inside |
| Switch (open) | Gap in the line |
| Switch (closed) | Line connecting both sides |
| Motor | Circle with M inside |
| Buzzer | Circle with a small rectangle |

### How Circuits Work
- Electricity needs a **complete loop** to flow.
- If the circuit is **broken** (open switch, loose wire), current stops.
- Components are powered by the **voltage** from cells/batteries.

### Changing Components
| Change | Effect |
|---|---|
| Add more cells | Bulb gets brighter (more voltage) |
| Add more bulbs in series | Each bulb gets dimmer (voltage shared) |
| Remove a bulb in series | Remaining bulbs get brighter |
| Open the switch | Circuit breaks, everything stops |

### Drawing Circuit Diagrams
Use the standard symbols, straight lines, and right angles. Make sure the loop is complete.

## Quick Quiz
1. What is voltage? → *The push that drives electricity around a circuit*
2. What happens if you add more cells? → *The bulb gets brighter*
3. What is a series circuit? → *A circuit with one loop*
4. What happens when a switch is open? → *The circuit breaks and current stops*

## Science in the Real World
Every electrical device — from phones to fridges — uses circuits. Engineers design circuits for safety, efficiency, and function.
