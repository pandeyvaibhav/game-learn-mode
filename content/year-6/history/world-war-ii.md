# World War II — Year 6 History

## Big Question
What caused World War II and how did it affect life in Britain?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about the **causes** of World War II, the **Blitz**, **evacuation**, the **home front**, and **VE Day**.

## Key History Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| Allies | countries that fought together against Germany (Britain, USA, USSR, France, etc.) |
| Axis | Germany, Italy, and Japan |
| evacuation | moving children from cities to the countryside for safety |
| Blitz | German bombing raids on British cities (1940-41) |
| home front | life for civilians during the war |
| VE Day | Victory in Europe Day, 8 May 1945 |

## The History Explained

### Causes
- Germany, led by Adolf Hitler, invaded Poland on 1 September 1939.
- Britain and France declared war on Germany on 3 September 1939.
- Hitler wanted to expand German territory and had already broken treaties.

### The Blitz
- From September 1940, German planes bombed London and other cities.
- People sheltered in Underground stations and Anderson shelters.
- The Blitz killed around 43,000 civilians and destroyed many buildings.

### Evacuation
- Over 1.5 million children were evacuated from cities to the countryside.
- Children carried gas masks and a small suitcase.
- Many had never left their city before — it was exciting and frightening.

### The Home Front
- **Rationing** — food, clothing, and fuel were limited.
- **Women** took on factory and farming jobs (e.g. Land Girls, munitions workers).
- **"Dig for Victory"** — families grew their own vegetables.
- **Blackout** — windows covered at night to hide cities from bombers.

### VE Day — 8 May 1945
- Germany surrendered.
- Street parties and celebrations across Britain.
- The war in the Pacific continued until August 1945.

## Quick Quiz
1. When did World War II start? → *1 September 1939 (Germany invaded Poland)*
2. What was the Blitz? → *German bombing raids on British cities*
3. Why were children evacuated? → *To keep them safe from bombing in cities*
4. What was VE Day? → *Victory in Europe Day, 8 May 1945*

## History in the Real World
World War II shaped the modern world — the United Nations, the European Union, and many international laws were created to prevent another global conflict.
