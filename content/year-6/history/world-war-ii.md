# World War II 🎯

## What We're Learning
World War II (1939–1945) was the largest conflict in human history and had a huge impact on Britain and the world. We'll explore why the war started, how it affected everyday life in Britain, and how it finally ended.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Blitz | The German bombing campaign against British cities in 1940–1941 |
| evacuation | Moving people, especially children, from cities to safer areas in the countryside |
| rationing | Limiting the amount of food and goods people could buy to ensure fair sharing |
| VE Day | Victory in Europe Day, 8th May 1945, when the war in Europe ended |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Causes of the War
After World War I, Germany faced harsh penalties under the Treaty of Versailles. Adolf Hitler and the Nazi Party rose to power in 1933, promising to restore Germany's strength. Hitler invaded neighbouring countries, and when Germany invaded Poland on 1st September 1939, Britain and France declared war.

### The Home Front
Life in Britain changed dramatically:
- **The Blitz (1940–41):** German planes bombed London and other cities for months. People sheltered in Anderson shelters in gardens and Underground stations. Over 40,000 civilians were killed.
- **Evacuation:** Around 1.5 million children were sent from cities to the countryside to keep them safe. Many had never left their home town before.
- **Rationing:** Food, clothing, and petrol were rationed so everyone got a fair share. Families grew vegetables in "Dig for Victory" gardens.

### The End of the War
The Allies (Britain, USA, Soviet Union, and others) fought back on multiple fronts. D-Day (6th June 1944) saw Allied troops land in Normandy, France, beginning the liberation of Europe. Germany surrendered on 8th May 1945 — celebrated as **VE Day**. The war in the Pacific ended in August 1945.

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** When did World War II begin and end?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>It began on 3rd September 1939 (when Britain declared war on Germany) and ended in Europe on 8th May 1945 (VE Day).</details>

**Question 2:** What was the Blitz?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>The Blitz was the sustained German bombing campaign against British cities, especially London, from September 1940 to May 1941. The aim was to destroy morale and force Britain to surrender.</details>

**Question 3:** Why were children evacuated from cities?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>To keep them safe from bombing raids. Children were sent to live with families in the countryside, away from the targeted cities.</details>

**Question 4:** What was rationing and why was it introduced?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Rationing limited how much food and goods each person could buy. It was introduced because supplies were scarce due to the war, and the government wanted to ensure fair distribution for everyone.</details>

**Question 5:** What was D-Day and why was it important?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>D-Day (6th June 1944) was the Allied invasion of Normandy, France. It was a turning point because it began the liberation of Western Europe from Nazi occupation and led towards the end of the war.</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
During the war, Bletchley Park in Buckinghamshire was home to Britain's codebreakers. Alan Turing and his team cracked the German "Enigma" code, which is estimated to have shortened the war by around two years and saved millions of lives.
