# Speeches & Debate — Year 6 English

## Big Question
How do we write and deliver a persuasive speech?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn **persuasive techniques**, **rhetorical devices**, how to structure a **debate**, and how to make a **rebuttal**.

## Key English Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| persuade | to convince someone to agree with your point of view |
| rhetoric | the art of using language effectively to persuade |
| rhetorical question | a question asked for effect, not expecting an answer |
| rule of three | listing three things for impact (e.g. "strong, brave, determined") |
| rebuttal | a counter-argument that challenges the other side |
| emotive language | words chosen to create an emotional response |

## The English Explained

### Speech Structure
1. **Opening** — grab attention (question, shocking fact, anecdote).
2. **Argument 1** — strongest point, with evidence.
3. **Argument 2** — second point, with evidence.
4. **Counter-argument & rebuttal** — acknowledge the other side, then disprove it.
5. **Conclusion** — call to action or powerful closing statement.

### Persuasive Techniques (AFOREST)
| Technique | Example |
|---|---|
| **A**lliteration | *Powerful, passionate people…* |
| **F**acts | *90% of scientists agree…* |
| **O**pinion (presented as fact) | *Everyone knows that…* |
| **R**epetition | *We must act. We must act now.* |
| **E**motive language | *Innocent children are suffering…* |
| **S**tatistics | *1 in 5 children…* |
| **T**hree (rule of) | *It is unfair, unjust, and unacceptable.* |

### Debate Rules
- **Proposition** speaks for the motion; **Opposition** speaks against.
- Each speaker presents arguments **with evidence**.
- Rebuttals should be **respectful and logical**.
- The audience votes at the end.

## Quick Quiz
1. What is a rhetorical question? → *A question asked for effect, not expecting an answer*
2. What does AFOREST stand for? → *Alliteration, Facts, Opinion, Repetition, Emotive language, Statistics, Three*
3. What is a rebuttal? → *A counter-argument challenging the other side*
4. How should a speech opening grab attention? → *With a question, fact, or anecdote*

## English in the Real World
Politicians, lawyers, activists, and business leaders use speeches and debate daily. Learning to argue persuasively and listen to opposing views is a vital life skill.
