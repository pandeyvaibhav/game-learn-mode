# Formal and Informal Writing 🎯

## What We're Learning
Good writers choose the right tone for their audience. A letter to the council sounds very different from a message to your best friend. We'll explore formal and informal registers and learn grammar features that help us write with authority.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| register | The level of formality in a piece of writing |
| formal | Professional, impersonal language used in official writing |
| informal | Casual, friendly language used with people you know well |
| subjunctive mood | A verb form used for wishes or suggestions (e.g. "If I were…") |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Formal Writing
Formal writing uses Standard English, avoids slang, and sounds professional. It's used for letters to officials, reports, essays, and news articles.

**Features:**
- Full forms: *"I am writing"* (not *"I'm writing"*)
- Passive voice: *"The decision was made"* (not *"We decided"*)
- Subjunctive mood: *"I suggest that he attend the meeting"*
- Formal connectives: *"Furthermore", "Consequently", "Nevertheless"*

### Informal Writing
Informal writing is chatty and personal. We use it for texts, emails to friends, diary entries, and dialogue in stories.

**Features:**
- Contractions: *"can't", "won't", "I'm"*
- Colloquial language: *"loads of", "cool", "hang out"*
- Short sentences, questions, exclamations
- First person and personal tone

### The Subjunctive and Passive Voice
The **subjunctive mood** is used for formal suggestions, demands, or hypothetical situations:
- *"If I **were** you…"* (not "was")
- *"The council requests that each resident **submit** a form."*

The **passive voice** removes the "doer" to sound impersonal:
- Active: *The chef cooked the meal.* → Passive: *The meal was cooked.*

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** Rewrite informally: "I am writing to inform you that your application has been successful."
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Hey! Great news — you got in!</details>

**Question 2:** Rewrite formally: "Can you fix the broken stuff in the park? It's really annoying."
<details><summary>Answer</summary>I am writing to request that the damaged equipment in the park be repaired at the earliest convenience.</details>

**Question 3:** Which is correct in formal writing: "If I was the prime minister" or "If I were the prime minister"?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>"If I were the prime minister" — this uses the subjunctive mood, which is correct for hypothetical situations in formal writing.</details>

**Question 4:** Change to passive voice: "The children planted 50 trees."
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Fifty trees were planted (by the children).</details>

**Question 5:** List three formal connectives you could use in an essay.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Furthermore, consequently, nevertheless (also: moreover, however, in addition, therefore).</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
The subjunctive mood is much more common in American English than British English. In the USA, you'll often hear "I suggest that he **go**" while in the UK, people usually say "I suggest that he **goes**."
