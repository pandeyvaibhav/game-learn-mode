# Formal & Informal Writing — Year 6 English

## Big Question
When should we write formally and when is informal writing appropriate?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn the difference between **formal** and **informal register**, how to choose the right one for your **audience** and **purpose**, and key language features of each.

## Key English Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| register | the level of formality in language |
| formal | serious, professional language for official situations |
| informal | relaxed, everyday language for friends and family |
| audience | who you are writing for |
| purpose | why you are writing (inform, persuade, entertain) |
| subjunctive | a formal verb form, e.g. "If I were…" or "I suggest that he go…" |

## The English Explained

### Formal vs Informal
| Feature | Formal | Informal |
|---|---|---|
| Tone | Serious, polite | Friendly, chatty |
| Contractions | No (do not, cannot) | Yes (don't, can't) |
| Slang | Never | Sometimes |
| Sentences | Longer, complex | Shorter, simpler |
| Pronouns | Third person often | First/second person |
| Examples | Letters to officials, reports | Texts to friends, diary entries |

### Formal Language Features
- **Passive voice**: *The decision was made…*
- **Subjunctive mood**: *If I were to suggest…*
- **Impersonal tone**: *It is believed that…*
- **Technical vocabulary**: use precise words.

### Informal Language Features
- **Contractions**: *I'm, we've, can't*
- **Colloquialisms**: *loads of, hang on*
- **Exclamations**: *What a great day!*
- **Direct address**: *You won't believe this…*

### Choosing the Right Register
Ask: **Who** am I writing for? **Why** am I writing?
- Letter to the council → **formal**
- Text to a friend → **informal**
- School newspaper article → **semi-formal**

## Quick Quiz
1. What is register? → *The level of formality in language*
2. Should a letter to the head teacher be formal or informal? → *Formal*
3. Give an example of a contraction. → *Don't / can't / I'm*
4. What is the subjunctive? → *A formal verb form, e.g. "If I were…"*

## English in the Real World
Knowing when to switch register is essential — job applications and emails to teachers need formal language, while messages to friends are informal. Getting it wrong can cause misunderstanding.

## Learning Checklist
- [ ] I can identify formal and informal language features
- [ ] I can choose the right register for audience and purpose
- [ ] I can use formal features like passive voice and subjunctive
