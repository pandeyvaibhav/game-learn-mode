# Fronted Adverbials — Year 4 English

## Big Question
What are fronted adverbials and how do they make our writing more interesting?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn what a **fronted adverbial** is, the different types (time, place, manner), and how to punctuate them with a **comma**.

## Key English Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| adverbial | a word or phrase that tells us how, when, or where something happens |
| fronted adverbial | an adverbial placed at the start of a sentence |
| comma | the punctuation mark used after a fronted adverbial |
| time adverbial | tells us when (e.g. Later that day, …) |
| place adverbial | tells us where (e.g. Behind the shed, …) |
| manner adverbial | tells us how (e.g. Carefully, …) |

## The English Explained

### What Is a Fronted Adverbial?
A fronted adverbial is a word or phrase at the **beginning** of a sentence that tells us **when**, **where**, or **how** something happened. It is always followed by a **comma**.

### Types
| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Time (when?) | *After breakfast, the children went outside.* |
| Place (where?) | *Behind the tall oak tree, a fox was hiding.* |
| Manner (how?) | *Silently, the cat crept across the room.* |

### The Comma Rule
> Fronted adverbial **,** rest of the sentence.

- ✅ *With great care, she painted the picture.*
- ❌ *With great care she painted the picture.* (missing comma!)

### More Examples
- *Before long, the sun began to set.*
- *At the bottom of the garden, there was a pond.*
- *As quick as a flash, he caught the ball.*

## Quick Quiz
1. What is a fronted adverbial? → *A word or phrase at the start of a sentence telling when, where, or how*
2. What punctuation follows a fronted adverbial? → *A comma*
3. "Behind the wall" is what type of adverbial? → *Place*
4. "After lunch" is what type of adverbial? → *Time*

## English in the Real World
Authors use fronted adverbials to add variety and detail to their writing. News reporters use them to set the scene: "Earlier today, the Prime Minister announced…"

## Learning Checklist
- [ ] I can identify a fronted adverbial in a sentence
- [ ] I know the three types: time, place, and manner
- [ ] I always use a comma after a fronted adverbial
