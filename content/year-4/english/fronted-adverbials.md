# Fronted Adverbials 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning about fronted adverbials — words or phrases placed at the beginning of a sentence to tell the reader when, where or how something happens. You'll practise spotting them, writing them and using a comma correctly afterwards.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| adverbial | A word or phrase that tells us when, where or how something happens |
| fronted | Placed at the front (start) of a sentence |
| comma | A punctuation mark (,) used after a fronted adverbial to separate it from the main clause |
| clause | A group of words containing a subject and a verb |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### What Is a Fronted Adverbial?
A fronted adverbial is an adverbial moved to the beginning of a sentence. It adds extra detail and makes writing more varied and interesting. A comma always follows it.

### Types of Fronted Adverbials
- **Time:** *Later that evening,* the fox crept out of its den.
- **Place:** *Beyond the mountains,* a hidden valley stretched for miles.
- **Manner (how):** *With great care,* she placed the egg back in the nest.

### Why Use Them?
Fronted adverbials stop your writing sounding the same. Compare:
- "The knight charged at the dragon bravely." → *Bravely, the knight charged at the dragon.*
- The second version grabs the reader's attention right away.

## Did You Know? 🌟
Fronted adverbials are a key feature tested in the Year 4 grammar assessment. Once you master them, your writing will sound much more like a published author's!
