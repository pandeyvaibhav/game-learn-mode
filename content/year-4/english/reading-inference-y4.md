# Reading Inference — Year 4 English

## Big Question
How can we work out what a character is thinking or feeling even when the author doesn't tell us directly?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn how to **infer** meaning from a text — reading between the lines to understand characters' **feelings**, **motives**, and **thoughts** by using **evidence** from the text.

## Key English Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| inference | working something out from clues in the text |
| evidence | the words or details from the text that support your idea |
| literal | information that is stated directly |
| implied | information that is suggested but not said directly |
| motive | the reason a character does something |

## The English Explained

### Literal vs Inference
| Literal (on the page) | Inference (between the lines) |
|---|---|
| "She slammed the door." | She is angry or upset. |
| "His hands were shaking." | He is scared or nervous. |
| "She looked at the floor." | She might feel guilty or shy. |

### How to Infer
1. **Read** the text carefully.
2. **Spot clues** — actions, dialogue, descriptions.
3. **Think** — what do these clues tell me?
4. **Prove it** — find the evidence in the text.

### PEE Structure for Answers
- **P**oint — state what you think.
- **E**vidence — quote from the text.
- **E**xplain — say how the evidence shows this.

> **Point:** Tom is nervous. **Evidence:** "His hands were shaking as he opened the letter." **Explain:** Shaking hands suggest he is worried about what the letter says.

## Quick Quiz
1. What does 'inference' mean? → *Working something out from clues in the text*
2. "She slammed the door." What can you infer? → *She is angry or upset*
3. What does PEE stand for? → *Point, Evidence, Explain*
4. What is the difference between literal and implied? → *Literal is stated directly; implied is suggested*

## English in the Real World
We use inference every day — reading body language, understanding jokes, and spotting sarcasm. Detectives use inference to solve crimes from clues!

- [ ] I can find clues in a text to infer feelings
