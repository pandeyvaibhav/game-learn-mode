# Persuasive Writing 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning how to write persuasively — that means trying to convince someone to agree with your point of view. You'll discover powerful techniques like rhetorical questions and emotive language that make your writing more convincing.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| persuade | To try to convince someone to think or do something |
| rhetorical question | A question asked for effect — you don't expect an answer |
| emotive language | Words chosen to make the reader feel a strong emotion |
| opinion | A personal belief that cannot be proved true or false |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Opinion vs Fact
A **fact** can be proved: "The school day starts at 8:50 a.m." An **opinion** is what someone thinks: "Lunchtime should be longer." Good persuasive writing uses both facts to back up your opinions.

### Rhetorical Questions
A rhetorical question makes the reader think without expecting an answer. For example: *Don't you deserve a better playground?* It pulls the reader onto your side.

### Emotive Language
Choosing words that stir feelings makes your argument stronger. Compare: "The park is untidy" with "The park is a disgusting, neglected mess." The second version creates a stronger reaction and pushes the reader to agree something must change.

**Question 1:** Is this a fact or an opinion? "Dogs are the best pets."
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Opinion — it is a personal belief and cannot be proved.</details>

**Question 2:** Write a rhetorical question to persuade people to recycle more.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Example: Shouldn't we all do our part to protect the planet?</details>

**Question 3:** Which sentence uses emotive language? (a) "The old tree was cut down." (b) "The beautiful, ancient tree was heartlessly destroyed."
<details><summary>Answer</summary>(b) — words like "beautiful", "ancient" and "heartlessly destroyed" stir emotions.</details>

**Question 4:** Give one reason why a writer might include facts in a persuasive text.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Facts make the argument more believable and support the writer's opinions with evidence.</details>

**Question 5:** Write a persuasive opening sentence about why children should have longer break times.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Example: Imagine a school where every child has enough time to run, play and recharge — wouldn't that make everyone happier and more ready to learn?</details>
