# Persuasive Writing — Year 4 English

## Big Question
How do we convince someone to agree with our point of view?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn the features of **persuasive writing**: giving your **opinion**, supporting it with **reasons**, using **rhetorical questions**, and choosing **persuasive language**.

## Key English Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| persuade | to convince someone to agree with you |
| opinion | what you think or believe |
| reason | why you think something — the evidence |
| rhetorical question | a question you don't expect an answer to |
| emotive language | words that create strong feelings |
| counter-argument | the opposite point of view |

## The English Explained

### Structure of Persuasive Writing
1. **Opening** — state your opinion clearly.
2. **Arguments** — give reasons with evidence.
3. **Counter-argument** — mention the other side and explain why you still disagree.
4. **Conclusion** — restate your opinion strongly.

### Persuasive Techniques
| Technique | Example |
|---|---|
| Rhetorical question | *Don't you think every child deserves a park?* |
| Emotive language | *Imagine the joy on children's faces…* |
| Facts and statistics | *9 out of 10 children prefer…* |
| Rule of three | *It is fun, healthy, and free!* |
| Connectives | *Furthermore… In addition… Most importantly…* |

### Example Opening
> **I strongly believe** that our school should have a longer lunchtime. **Wouldn't you agree** that children need more time to play, eat, and relax?

### Useful Persuasive Phrases
- I firmly believe…
- Surely you would agree that…
- It is clear that…
- Without a doubt…
- The evidence shows…

## Quick Quiz
1. What is a rhetorical question? → *A question you don't expect an answer to*
2. Name one persuasive technique. → *Emotive language / rhetorical question / rule of three / facts*
3. What is a counter-argument? → *The opposite point of view*
4. What should the conclusion do? → *Restate your opinion strongly*

## English in the Real World
Persuasive writing is used in adverts, speeches, debates, newspaper editorials, and charity campaigns. Politicians, lawyers, and marketers all use persuasion every day.

## Learning Checklist
- [ ] I can state my opinion and support it with reasons
- [ ] I can use persuasive techniques (rhetorical questions, emotive language, rule of three)
- [ ] I can structure a piece of persuasive writing
