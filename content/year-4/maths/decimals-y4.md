# Decimals 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning about decimals — numbers that sit between whole numbers. You'll find out what tenths and hundredths are, how to write them using a decimal point, and how they connect to fractions you already know.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| decimal | A number that uses a decimal point to show parts smaller than one |
| tenth | One of ten equal parts — written as 0.1 or 1/10 |
| hundredth | One of a hundred equal parts — written as 0.01 or 1/100 |
| equivalent | Different ways of showing the same value, e.g. 0.5 = 1/2 |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Tenths
If you divide 1 whole into 10 equal parts, each part is one tenth. We write this as 0.1 or 1/10. Three tenths is 0.3, and ten tenths make 1 whole (1.0).

### Hundredths
If you divide 1 whole into 100 equal parts, each part is one hundredth (0.01). There are 10 hundredths in every tenth. So 0.10 is the same as 0.1, and 0.25 means 2 tenths and 5 hundredths.

### Decimals and Fractions
Many fractions have simple decimal equivalents:
- 1/2 = 0.5
- 1/4 = 0.25
- 3/4 = 0.75
- 1/10 = 0.1

Understanding these links helps you move between fractions and decimals easily.

## Did You Know? 🌟
Decimals are used every day with money! £2.50 means 2 pounds and 50 hundredths of a pound — which is the same as 2 pounds and 50 pence.
