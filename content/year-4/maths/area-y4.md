# Area — Year 4 Maths

## Big Question
How do we measure how much space a flat shape covers?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn what **area** is, how to measure it by **counting squares**, and how to calculate the area of rectangles.

## Key Maths Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| area | the amount of space inside a flat shape |
| square centimetre (cm²) | the standard unit for measuring area |
| length | how long a shape is |
| width | how wide a shape is |
| rectangle | a shape with 4 right angles and opposite sides equal |
| grid | a pattern of squares used to count area |

## The Maths Explained

### What Is Area?
Area is the space **inside** a flat shape. We measure it in **square centimetres (cm²)**.

### Counting Squares

3 rows × 4 columns = **12 cm²**

### Formula for Rectangles
**Area = length × width**

Example: a rectangle 5 cm long and 3 cm wide:
Area = 5 × 3 = **15 cm²**

### Irregular Shapes
For shapes that don't fit neatly on a grid:
1. Count the **full** squares.
2. Count squares that are **more than half** filled as 1.
3. Ignore squares that are **less than half** filled.

### Comparing Areas
A shape can have the **same perimeter** but different area (and vice versa). Always measure both to be sure.

## Quick Quiz
1. What unit do we use for area? → *Square centimetres (cm²)*
2. A rectangle is 6 cm by 4 cm. What is the area? → *24 cm²*
3. How do you find the area of a rectangle? → *Length × width*
4. How do you find the area of an irregular shape? → *Count the squares on a grid*

## Maths in the Real World
Area is used to work out the size of rooms, gardens, football pitches, and even countries. Painters need to know area to buy the right amount of paint.
