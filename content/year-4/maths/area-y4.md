# Area 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning what area means and how to measure it. You'll find out how to work out the area of shapes by counting squares and why we measure area in square centimetres (cm²).

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| area | The amount of space inside a flat shape |
| square centimetre | A square that is 1 cm long and 1 cm wide — written cm² |
| rectilinear | A shape made up of straight lines and right angles |
| grid | A pattern of squares used to measure area |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### What Is Area?
Area is the amount of flat space a shape covers. Imagine painting a wall — the area tells you how much paint you need. We measure area in **square units**, most often **square centimetres (cm²)**.

### Counting Squares
The simplest way to find area is to draw the shape on a grid and count the squares inside it. Each full square = 1 cm². For example, a rectangle that is 4 cm long and 3 cm wide covers 12 squares, so its area is 12 cm².

### Rectilinear Shapes
Rectilinear shapes are made of rectangles joined together (like an L-shape or T-shape). To find the area, you can split the shape into separate rectangles, find each rectangle's area, then add them together.

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** A rectangle is 5 cm long and 2 cm wide. What is its area?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>10 cm² (5 × 2 = 10)</details>

**Question 2:** A square has sides of 4 cm. What is its area?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>16 cm² (4 × 4 = 16)</details>

**Question 3:** An L-shape is made from a 3 × 2 rectangle and a 2 × 2 rectangle joined together. What is its total area?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>10 cm² (6 + 4 = 10)</details>

**Question 4:** Why do we measure area in square centimetres rather than just centimetres?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Because area measures a flat space in two directions (length and width), so we use a unit that covers a square of space, not just a line.</details>

**Question 5:** A rectangle has an area of 24 cm² and is 6 cm long. How wide is it?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>4 cm (24 ÷ 6 = 4)</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
A football pitch has an area of about 7,140 square metres — that's over 71 million square centimetres!
