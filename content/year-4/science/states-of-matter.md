# States of Matter — Year 4 Science

## Big Question
What are the three states of matter and how can materials change between them?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn about **solids**, **liquids**, and **gases**, how their particles behave, and the processes that change one state into another.

## Key Science Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| solid | a material with a fixed shape that does not flow |
| liquid | a material that flows and takes the shape of its container |
| gas | a material that spreads out to fill all available space |
| melting | changing from a solid to a liquid (by heating) |
| freezing | changing from a liquid to a solid (by cooling) |
| evaporation | changing from a liquid to a gas |
| condensation | changing from a gas to a liquid |

## The Science Explained

### The Three States
| State | Shape | Can it flow? | Can it be squashed? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Solid | Fixed | No | Very difficult |
| Liquid | Takes container shape | Yes | Very difficult |
| Gas | No fixed shape | Yes (spreads out) | Easy |

### Particles
- **Solid** — particles packed tightly, vibrate in place.
- **Liquid** — particles close but can slide past each other.
- **Gas** — particles far apart, move quickly in all directions.

### Changing State
| Process | Change | Cause |
|---|---|---|
| Melting | Solid → Liquid | Heating |
| Freezing | Liquid → Solid | Cooling |
| Evaporation | Liquid → Gas | Heating |
| Condensation | Gas → Liquid | Cooling |

### The Water Cycle (quick link)
Water naturally moves through all three states: ice (solid) → water (liquid) → water vapour (gas) and back again.

## Quick Quiz
1. Name the three states of matter. → *Solid, liquid, gas*
2. What is melting? → *Changing from a solid to a liquid by heating*
3. What is condensation? → *Changing from a gas to a liquid by cooling*
4. Can a gas be squashed easily? → *Yes*

## Science in the Real World
Cooking uses changes of state every day — ice melts, water boils, steam condenses on a lid. Factories melt metals to shape them, and freezing preserves food.
