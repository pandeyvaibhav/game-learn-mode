# States of Matter 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning that all materials exist as solids, liquids or gases, and that they can change from one state to another when heated or cooled. You'll explore what happens when ice melts, water boils, and puddles disappear on a sunny day.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| solid | A material with a fixed shape that stays in one place |
| liquid | A material that flows and takes the shape of its container |
| gas | A material that spreads out to fill all available space |
| evaporation | When a liquid changes into a gas, often at the surface |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Solids, Liquids and Gases
- **Solids** have a fixed shape and volume — like a book, a rock or an ice cube.
- **Liquids** have a fixed volume but take the shape of their container — like water, milk or juice.
- **Gases** have no fixed shape or volume — they spread everywhere, like the air around you.

### Changing State
Materials can change state:
- **Melting:** solid → liquid (e.g. ice melts to water at 0 °C)
- **Freezing:** liquid → solid (e.g. water freezes to ice at 0 °C)
- **Evaporation:** liquid → gas (e.g. puddles drying up)
- **Condensation:** gas → liquid (e.g. steam on a cold mirror)

### The Water Cycle Connection
Evaporation and condensation are key parts of the water cycle. The Sun heats water in rivers and oceans, turning it into water vapour (a gas). The vapour rises, cools and condenses into tiny droplets that form clouds, which eventually fall as rain.

## Try It Yourself! ✏️

**Question 1:** Name the three states of matter.
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Solid, liquid and gas.</details>

**Question 2:** What is it called when a liquid turns into a gas?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Evaporation (or boiling, if heated to boiling point).</details>

**Question 3:** At what temperature does water freeze?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>0 °C.</details>

**Question 4:** You breathe on a cold window and it mists up. What change of state is happening?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Condensation — the warm water vapour in your breath is cooling and turning into tiny liquid droplets on the glass.</details>

**Question 5:** Is toothpaste a solid, liquid or gas?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>Toothpaste can be tricky! It behaves a bit like both a solid and a liquid, but scientists usually describe it as a very thick liquid (or a gel).</details>

## Did You Know? 🌟
Chocolate is a solid at room temperature but melts at about 34 °C — just below body temperature. That's why it melts so easily in your mouth!
