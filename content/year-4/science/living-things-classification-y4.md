# Living Things Classification — Year 4 Science

## Big Question
How do scientists sort living things into groups?

## What We're Learning
In this lesson you will learn how to group living things using **classification keys**, the difference between **vertebrates** and **invertebrates**, and how living things are suited to their **environments**.

## Key Science Words
| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| classify | to sort living things into groups based on features |
| classification key | a set of yes/no questions that help identify a living thing |
| vertebrate | an animal with a backbone |
| invertebrate | an animal without a backbone |
| environment | the place where a living thing lives |
| habitat | the natural home of a living thing |

## The Science Explained

### Why Classify?
There are millions of living things on Earth. Scientists sort them into groups to make them easier to study and identify.

### Vertebrates (5 groups)
| Group | Features | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Mammals | Warm-blooded, fur/hair, live young | Dog |
| Birds | Warm-blooded, feathers, lay eggs | Robin |
| Reptiles | Cold-blooded, scales, lay eggs | Lizard |
| Amphibians | Cold-blooded, moist skin, lay eggs in water | Frog |
| Fish | Cold-blooded, scales, gills, live in water | Salmon |

### Invertebrates
Animals **without** a backbone — insects, spiders, snails, worms, jellyfish. Over 95% of all animals are invertebrates!

### Classification Keys
A classification key uses **yes/no questions** to identify a living thing step by step:
1. Does it have legs? → Yes → go to Q2 / No → it might be a worm or snake
2. Does it have six legs? → Yes → it is an insect / No → go to Q3
3. Does it have eight legs? → Yes → it is a spider / No → keep going…

### Environments
Living things are found in environments suited to their needs — a fish needs water, a cactus needs dry heat.

## Quick Quiz
1. What is a vertebrate? → *An animal with a backbone*
2. Name the five groups of vertebrates. → *Mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, fish*
3. What is a classification key? → *A set of yes/no questions to identify a living thing*
4. Are insects vertebrates or invertebrates? → *Invertebrates*

## Science in the Real World
Scientists use classification to discover new species, protect endangered animals, and understand how life on Earth is connected. Apps can even identify plants from a photo using digital classification keys!
