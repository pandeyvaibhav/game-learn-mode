# Living Things and Classification 🎯

## What We're Learning
We are learning how scientists group living things to make them easier to study. You'll use classification keys to identify animals and plants, and explore the difference between vertebrates and invertebrates.

## Key Words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| classification | Sorting living things into groups based on their features |
| classification key | A series of yes/no questions used to identify a living thing |
| vertebrate | An animal with a backbone (e.g. fish, bird, mammal) |
| invertebrate | An animal without a backbone (e.g. insect, spider, worm) |

## Let's Explore! 🔍

### Why Classify?
There are millions of species on Earth. Grouping them by shared features helps scientists study, name and understand them. The broadest groups are **plants** and **animals**, but each can be split further.

### Vertebrates and Invertebrates
Animals are first split into two big groups:
- **Vertebrates** have a backbone — fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds and mammals.
- **Invertebrates** have no backbone — insects, spiders, snails, worms and many more. About 97 % of all animal species are invertebrates!

### Using Classification Keys
A classification key asks questions like "Does it have wings? Yes → go to question 3. No → go to question 4." By following the questions you can identify an unknown creature step by step. You can also create your own keys.

## Did You Know? 🌟
The blue whale is the largest vertebrate ever to have lived, while the fairyfly wasp — an invertebrate — is so tiny it could sit on the tip of a pencil!
